# Zaryth's Speech — The Bakunawa's Rest

> **Occasion:** Return of the singing fleet. Celebration at the Bahay Kubo, Dagat-Puso.
> **Date:** Day 669, 29th Thawsend, 1255 AS
> **Audience:** ~350 — 131 singers, Datu council, Deep Fishers, Babaylan, Dawnwatch, citizens of Dagat-Puso
> **Language:** Wikang Dagat (delivered by Zaryth without translator)
> **DM:** Aldric Voss, The Chronicler (Session 11)

---

"Kapuluan. Mga kapatid. Brothers and sisters of the sea."

"Six months ago, we planted a seed on a seamount north of your waters. We did not know it would wake the Bakunawa. We did not know the sound would reach the trench. We came here to learn from you, and the first thing we did was cause harm."

"That is our responsibility. We own it. And today, with your help — with YOUR voices, YOUR courage, YOUR songs — we answered for it."

*[To the coral-singers]*

"To the coral-singers of Gitna ng Dagat — you held the ocean's frequency for three hours without faltering. You are the foundation. Without you, there is no song. The Bakunawa heard your voice first, and it recognized home."

*[To Orindel and Guro Awit]*

"To Orindel Ashwick and Guro Awit — you wrote a song that has never existed before. A bridge between two worlds. Orindel brought the Root's voice. Guro Awit brought fifty years of coral-singing. Together, you created something neither could have made alone. That is what Bayanihan means."

*[To the Deep Fishers]*

"To Bulan and the Deep Fishers of Timog Dulo — you put your faces in the water and sang into the dark. You carried the song where no one else could reach. You have watched the Bakunawa your whole lives. You have lost family to its waking. And today, you sang it to peace. Not with weapons. With your voices. You are the bravest people I have ever met."

*[To the Babaylan]*

"To the Babaylan — you sang without words. You gave the song its heart. Peace. Safety. Rest. The Bakunawa did not hear a command to sleep. It heard a promise that it was safe. That promise came from you."

*[To Kapitan Habagat]*

"To Kapitan Habagat — you sailed seven days at eighty-two years old because the sea asked. You stood in your boat and sang one note. One note that held a hundred and thirty voices together. The sea knows your voice. It has known it for sixty years. Today, your voice told the sea that everything was going to be all right. And the sea believed you."

*[To Datu Malaya]*

"To Datu Malaya — you called the Bayanihan. You gathered every singer in Kapuluan. You trusted strangers from across the ocean with your people's greatest crisis. That trust was the hardest thing anyone did today. Harder than singing. Harder than sailing. You trusted, and your people are safe because of it."

*[To the room]*

"We came to Kapuluan as strangers. You welcomed us with 'Mabuhay' — you are alive, we are glad you are here. You fed us with your hands. You taught us your crafts. You cared for my wife and my unborn daughter. And when the crisis came — a crisis WE caused — you did not blame us. You said: Bayanihan. When one needs help, all help."

"I have traveled two continents. I have met ten civilizations. I have spoken to a planetary intelligence and an alien castaway and a forest that learned to talk. And I tell you: Kapuluan is the most extraordinary place I have ever been. Not because of the coral. Not because of the boats. Because of the people. Because you chose to sing when you could have fought. Because you chose to trust when you could have turned us away."

"The Bakunawa sleeps. The reef heals. The outer islands can go home. And the Dawnwatch will carry the name of Kapuluan — and the word Bayanihan — to every corner of every continent we touch. Your story will be told. Your song will be remembered. Your courage will be honored."

*[Fist on sternum — Dawnwatch gesture. Hands open outward — Kapuluan gesture.]*

**"Maraming salamat. Mabuhay, Kapuluan."**
