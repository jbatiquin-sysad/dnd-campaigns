Zaryth spoke.

Not a command. Not a threat. He spoke the way he spoke to Thessara Kain in the chamber beneath Aelindra. 
The way he spoke to Calder at the Salted Crown. The way he spoke to Edric in the council chamber.

"Callista. The array is gone. The cylinders are in our hands. The fleet is chained. Sulwick is in 
custody. Dray is in custody. Your operatives have surrendered. There is no one left to give that order 
to. There is no one left to receive it.

"You are holding a key over a box that will kill you and everyone in this room. And for what? The 
Unchained Compact's mission is over. The Partition Writ is safe. The Tome is inert. The Binding Shard 
housing is secured. The ward network stands. Every sealed thing that was meant to open — stays sealed.

"You can turn that key and die in a collapsing building with a sergeant, two halflings, a rogue, and a 
Drow who has been walking across this country for a month trying to do the right thing. Or you can put 
the key down and walk out of this room alive.

"I have watched people choose redemption in five cities. I have watched a solicitor burn his own evidence
to protect a young man's future. I have watched an Archivist-General surrender her life's work because a
friend asked her to. I have watched a brother testify against his own family.

"You are not the worst person I have met on this road. You are not even close. You are someone who 
believed in something and followed it too far. That is not unforgivable. That is human.

"Put the key down, Callista. Let this be the last room where someone has to make this choice."
