# Toast on the *Eilistraee's Grace*

## Scene
**23rd Ashfall, 1249 AS — Deck of the *Eilistraee's Grace*, at sea, heading home**

The sun sets behind the western coast. Orange light on calm water. The resonance drive hums low. Lyriel's cut the speed to cruising. For the first time in twenty-two days, nobody is running toward something or away from something.

Seren cooks on the ship's brazier — rice, fresh mackerel, wild garlic, lemon. Lyriel produces a bottle of Vel'quinar moonwine from the captain's locker. Her mother made it. The only bottle in the world.

Seven tin cups. Mismatched. Borrowed from the galley. The wine is pale silver. It catches the last light and holds it.

Zaryth stands. The deck rocks gently. His team around him.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

"Three weeks ago we were eating Pip's eggs and planning our next ward repair. Tonight we're on a ship with a three-thousand-year-old Binder in the hold, six inverted Heartstones in a lead crate, two new allies, a dismantled spy network, and a story that nobody is going to believe."

He lets the laughter settle.

"We've been running since Day 181. The scouts. The ambush. Operation Copper Hair — Nhilara carried a seven-year-old girl through a hostile city in the dark and didn't make a sound. The sweep — two cities in one night. Ashport. The workshop. The Anchor Point. Vaelen's voice in the stone. And today — a woman who's been alive since before history started, taken down on a foggy pier by the best people I've ever known."

He looks at each of them.

"Mira. You haven't been seen in three weeks. Literally. You fired the first shot at a Binder and she never knew where you were. That's not luck. That's you."

Mira raises her cup from the rope coil. Doesn't speak. Doesn't need to.

"Thessaly. You walked into a chandlery, a Veldmere bank, and a war zone in the same week, and you came out of all three with a profit. The two thousand gold is just the part we can count."

Thessaly: "I'm keeping the spice ledger."

"Seren. You punched two guards unconscious at dawn, held the cove mouth alone, took a Ward Pulse that would have killed most people, and pulled yourself out of the ocean to keep fighting. Thirty-one years alone made you into something the world wasn't ready for."

Seren lifts her cup. Rice grain on her chin. She doesn't wipe it.

"Orindel. You kept us alive. Every time someone went down, you were there. Healing Word, arrow, hymn — whatever was needed. And after the fight, you sat with Thessara and didn't say a word. That's the hardest medicine there is."

Orindel nods. His eyes are bright.

"Nhilara. You came out of the sand like something from a nightmare and you held the line against a Binder while the rest of us were picking ourselves up off the ground. You're the reason she didn't run. You're the reason we won."

Nhilara turns from the stern. The wake stretches behind them in the fading light. She raises her cup. "To the future. Not the past."

"Lyriel. Dren. You sailed us into a smuggler's cove, held position for days, and had the engine running when we needed to leave. The *Grace* is the reason any of this was possible."

Lyriel: "She's a good ship. She deserves a better name than the one I gave her."

Dren, quietly: "The name is perfect."

He raises his cup higher. The silver wine catches starlight.

"To the Dawnwatch. To the ones at home — Aldric holding the wall, Bram at the forge, Pip on the tower, Sylvara with her notebook, Torven in the deep. To Vaelith, who's been waiting a thousand years for this to matter. To Ilyana, who broke a ring to save her son and built a house from nothing. To Tessa and the kids. To Velyndra and Taelar and Sylla, who chose glass and weaving over fear."

"To Vaelen, who's been singing alone in the dark for three thousand years. We're coming back for you."

"And to whatever's on the other side of the door. If they're still there — if they're still listening — maybe it's time someone knocked back."

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

He drinks. They all drink.

Drow moonwine tastes like cold silver and starlight and something that doesn't have a name in Common. It tastes like the thing you didn't know you were missing until you found it.

Mira: "...that's really good."

Thessaly: "Lyriel, is there more?"

Lyriel: "That was the only bottle in the world."

Thessaly: "Then I'm glad it was tonight."
