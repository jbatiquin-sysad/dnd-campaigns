# Orindel: The Vigil

The stone remembers what the sky forgot —
a single voice where thousands rang.
One hundred years the warden watched alone.
One hundred years the silence sang.

Then eight came walking up the ancient road,
with rings of steel and hearts of flame.
They spoke to what the darkness held at bay
and called the singing back by name.

Light, light, the ward-light rising —
stone to stone the old road knows.
What was broken finds its mending.
What was silent — sings — and grows.
