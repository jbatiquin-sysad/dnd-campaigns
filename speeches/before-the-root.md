Zaryth stood. Looked at each of them. Took his time.

"When I met you, I was a man carrying a ruined ring from a fallen house, walking into a tavern at midnight because I 
had nowhere else to go. I had a sword and a name that meant nothing and a belief that one person could still make a 
difference — even when every piece of evidence said otherwise.

"I was wrong about one thing. One person cannot make a difference. Eight people can.

"Mira — you taught me that freedom is not something you buy back. It is something you decide you already have. You 
decided it in a warehouse at midnight, and you have decided it every day since.

"Aldric — you held a forge coal in your chest for 14 minutes because someone needed you to. You have held all of us 
together the same way, every day, without complaint and without rest.

"Sylvara — last night you taught us the history of the world. But you have been teaching us something more important 
since Duskport: that knowledge without courage is just words on a shelf. You left everything you knew to do what was 
right. That is the bravest kind of wisdom.

"Bram — you carried 120 pounds of brass through dark alleys. You carried orders to Duskport when we sailed to Aelindra.
You carried this party's discipline on your shoulders without ever being asked. You are the most reliable person I 
have ever known.

"Thessaly — you kept a list of seven names and you crossed out every one. Not for vengeance. For memory. For the people
who were wronged and the cities that deserved better. You held gates and councils and courtrooms with the same steady 
hand. You are the spine of the Dawnwatch.

"Orindel — you healed with your hands when you had magic and with your presence when you didn't. You named our birds 
after the people we saved. You carry honey and whiskey and a carved icon from every city because you believe that gifts
matter as much as swords. You are right.

"Pip — you named us. You held an anchor with a river pebble in your fist. You wrote a letter to a girl you spoke to for
ten minutes and sealed it with our ring. You are not an accident. You never were. You are the heart of the Dawnwatch, 
and I am proud to walk beside you.

"We started with a debt in a tavern. We end — or we begin again — at the Root of the world. We did not come this far by
strength or by cunning. We came this far because in every city, in every dark room, in every moment where it would 
have been easier to look away, we chose not to.

"A new dawn is approaching. Not just for us. For the Archive. For the six cities that trusted us. For the sealed places
that have been sleeping for 200 years, waiting for someone to come and ask — not command — if they are ready.

"We could not have done this without every person who helped us. Vael Orun, who waited ten years. Cassin Vael, who 
closed an account. Edric, who testified against his own blood. Harwick, who burned his files and kept his conscience. 
Veyra, who held an institution together with her bare hands. Thessara, who chose redemption. Serevane, who chose 
defection. Revka, who chose her brother. Duvane, who chose enough. Every guard captain who laid down his sword. Every 
miner who picked up a lantern. Every clerk who kept copies. Every child who watched wagons in the dark.

"We carry them with us. All of them. Down the stairs. To the door. To the Root.

"The Dawnwatch does not command. The Dawnwatch asks. And today we ask the oldest thing in the world if it is ready for 
a new dawn.

"Onward. Together. One last time."
