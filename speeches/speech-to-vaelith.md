# Speech to Vaelith

## Scene
**18th Highsun, 1247 AS — Greyveil Pass, The Meeting**

The wind dies. Just for a moment. As if the mountain itself is listening.

Zaryth steps forward. He doesn't kneel — this isn't a lord. He doesn't salute — this isn't a commander. He stands as an equal. One warden to another.

"We have come to help. We are deeply honored by your invitation."

He places his hand on his chest. The Dawnwatch signet ring catches the grey light.

"I am Zaryth Vel'quinar. I am a Drow who was exiled for refusing to harm the innocent. I walked out of the Underdark with nothing, and I found seven people who chose to walk beside me."

He turns to each of them.

"Mira Thistlewick. She came for the money and stayed for something she still won't name. She is the reason we see danger before it sees us."

Mira nods once. Barefoot on the cold stone. Steady.

"Brother Aldric Stonebrow. A healer whose temple was destroyed by the very necromancy you sought to use. He carried that grief alone for years. He is the reason we know that the boundary between life and death is sacred — and worth protecting by other means."

Aldric meets Vaelith's eyes. Dwarf to ancient. The weight between them is visible.

"Sylvara Dawnwhisper. She mapped every conspiracy, every ward, every sealed site we've encountered. She reconstructed your repair ritual from fragments in the Archive. She is the reason we stand here knowing what Node 12 needs."

Sylvara inclines her head. The Ward Compass hums in Zaryth's hand.

"Bram Thistlewick. A halfling soldier who taught himself magic from a dead wizard's spellbook through sheer stubbornness. He built our table, our forge, and our home. He is the reason we have something worth defending."

Bram stands with his arms crossed. Three feet tall. Immovable.

"Thessaly Ironveil. She walks into any room and owns it. She dismantled a seven-target conspiracy list across six cities with nothing but her voice and her nerve. She is the reason our enemies never see us coming."

Thessaly smiles. It's the dangerous one.

"Pip Tanglethorn. He stole a spellbook on a dare and set a barn on fire. He held an arc of the Valis Procedure for fourteen minutes without flickering. He lights up every room he enters — literally. He is the reason we remember to laugh."

Pip waves. Then looks embarrassed. Then waves again.

"Orindel Ashwick. He picks pockets and donates to orphanages. He quotes scripture to justify everything. He heard your ward singing through the stone road last night and knew what it meant. He is the reason we never forget what we're fighting for."

Orindel places his hand over his holy symbol. A quiet bow.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Zaryth turns back to Vaelith.

"We are the Dawnwatch. We renewed the Root covenant. We dismantled the Unchained Compact across seven cities. We captured the necromancer who destroyed Aldric's temple. We captured the broker who funded her. We sealed two of your barrows. And we walked the stone road your order built, past wardens who are still standing guard after a thousand years."

His voice doesn't rise. It doesn't need to.

"We understand the crisis. We have seen the compass readings. We know Node 12 is at twenty-eight percent. We know the cascade is real. We know what is on the other side of that ward."

He pauses.

"But we also know this: for every crisis, there is a solution. And that solution does not require sacrificing the innocent. It does not require raising the dead. The people buried in those barrows trusted that their rest would be honored. The seventeen brothers and sisters of Aldric's temple trusted that the boundary would hold. We will not break that trust — not for expedience, not for fear, not for any reason."

He looks at the flickering ward behind Vaelith. The thin glass between the living world and whatever waits beyond.

"You have carried this burden alone for a very long time. Longer than anyone should. You watched the wards decay. You watched the order die. You stood here, year after year, with no one coming. And when you finally called for help, the only answer you could imagine was the dead."

"We are here to offer a different answer."

"Let us share this burden. Let us repair the wards — together. Your knowledge and ours. The ritual you carry and the one Sylvara reconstructed. The cache your order left behind and the hands willing to use it."

"You don't have to do this alone anymore."

Silence. The wind returns, but gently. The ward flickers behind Vaelith like a heartbeat.

The ancient warden stares at Zaryth for a long time. The burning eyes move across the party — each face, each stance, each signet ring. Eight people who walked into the mountains because they were asked.

Something shifts in Vaelith's expression. Not softening — releasing. Like a fist held closed for a thousand years, finally opening.

Their voice, when it comes, is quieter than before. The grinding stone smoothed to something almost human.

"I have stood at this pass for one hundred and fourteen years. Before that, I walked the stone road for nine hundred more. I am the last. Every brother and sister of the Vigil is gone. Every warden has fallen or faded. I maintained what I could. I watched what I couldn't."

"When the cascade began, I did what I was trained to do — I activated the contingency. The dead. It was in our protocols. A last resort written by people who never imagined it would be needed. I followed the manual because there was no one left to tell me not to."

They look at Aldric.

"I did not know about your temple. I did not choose Veradis for what she did at Ashenmere. I chose her for what she could do. That was my failure. I see it now."

Aldric says nothing. But he nods. Once. It's enough.

Vaelith plants the staff in the stone. The ward symbols flare blue.

"You carry the compass. You walked the road. The wardens let you pass. That has not happened in a thousand years."

They reach into their robe and withdraw a small leather-bound book. Old. Worn. The closed eye embossed on the cover.

"The activation sequence. The missing piece of the ritual. I wrote it down when my memory began to fail. Take it."

Sylvara steps forward. Her hands are trembling. She takes the book. Opens it. Her eyes widen.

"This is it. This completes the ritual. With this and the cache components, we can repair Node 12."

Vaelith looks at Zaryth one final time.

"The cache is through the pass. Fifty paces past the ward line, in a vault beneath the closed eye stone. The resonance crystal is inside. The manual is inside. Everything the Vigil left for the ones who would come after."

"I will take you there. But beyond the ward line, the dead zone begins. You will feel it. Stay close. Move quickly. Do not linger."

They turn toward the pass. The flickering ward. The darkness beyond.

"You said you came to share the burden. Then walk with me. One last time."
