"I want to speak to the people of Salthollow — not as a commander, not as an outsider, but as 
someone who has seen what you built here and what was nearly taken from you.

Professor Dunwick. You were removed from your post for telling the truth. You spent two years 
in a house on Chandler's Row with a drawer full of evidence and a red pen, waiting for someone 
to listen. You didn't stop. You didn't leave. You didn't give up. That is not stubbornness — 
that is courage. The kind that doesn't make noise. The kind that just refuses to go away.

Councilwoman Ashworth. You sat in a committee with no budget and no staff, and you kept a 
ledger of every payment that shouldn't have been made. You did this knowing that the people who
bought your colleagues could buy your silence too — and you chose to be expensive. Last night,
you put your hand on a seal that hadn't been opened in two hundred years, and your town 
answered. That is what civic authority looks like when it remembers what it's for.

Dak. You carried crates you weren't supposed to look inside. You looked anyway. You were afraid
— and you stood up. There is no braver thing a person can do than be afraid and stand up 
anyway.

Senna."

She flinched. Looked up.

"You made a mistake. You reported on us because you were scared and you didn't know what else 
to do. And then, when someone offered you a door, you walked through it. You told the truth. 
You helped us. That took more courage than you think — because it's easy to be brave when you'
ve never been afraid. It's hard to be brave when you have been. Remember that."

Senna's chin trembled. She didn't look away.

"Lira. You wrote a note on a barge to strangers you'd never met, because you remembered what a 
university is for. You are the reason we knew what was happening in time to stop it. One note. 
One student. That was enough.

To everyone here — the faculty who stayed, the students who watched, the clerk who answered 
honestly, the woman who opened her boarding house without asking questions — you are the reason
Salthollow is still Salthollow. We did not save this town. You saved this town. We just helped
you finish what you started.

Four cities now stand together. Duskport. Harrowgate. Greymoor. Salthollow. Four cities that 
said: not here. Not us. Not anymore.

We are moving on tonight. There are people in Fenhollow who are waiting for help they don't 
know is coming. But I want you to know — what you did here will outlast all of us. The charters
beneath your feet have survived two hundred years. They will survive two hundred more. Because
people like you will guard them.

Thank you. All of you. It has been an honor.
