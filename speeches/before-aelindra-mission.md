"Before we go further — I need you to hear me. Not as your commander. As someone who has walked beside you and 
seen who you are.

Six days ago, we stepped off a ship into a city we did not know, with a mission most people would call 
impossible. And in those six days, every single one of you became something more than you were.

Mira — you walked into a city built for visibility and made yourself invisible anyway. You mapped every corridor,
timed every guard, and built us a road through the dark. You told me once that you didn't know who you were 
beyond running jobs. I know who you are. You are the reason we will reach that chamber tonight.

Aldric — you volunteered to hold a grounding circle that will burn through your body for eleven minutes. You didn
't hesitate. You didn't ask what it would cost. You said 'I'll hold.' That is not duty. That is love — for people
you have never met and will never know. The people sleeping above us tonight are alive tomorrow because of what 
you are about to do.

Sylvara — you came back to the place that shaped you, knowing it might break your heart. You walked into those 
Stacks and did not flinch. You taught Pip. You trained Aldric. You prepared Orindel. You carried the weight of 
knowing your Archivist-General — someone you respected — chose to become the thing you must now stop. And you are
still here. Still steady. Still the strongest person in this company.

Bram — you received a message on a scrap of paper tied to a pigeon's leg. It said come. And you came. Three days 
on a ship that smelled like fish, into a mission you learned about an hour ago, and you sat down and said 'I'm 
ready.' You have always been ready. You just needed someone to ask.

Pip — you carried a message tube across Duskport six months ago because a stranger asked you to. You didn't know 
what it was. You didn't know what it meant. But that small act — that ordinary, unremarkable act of a young 
halfling doing a job — set everything in motion. Everything. The Partition Writ. Maret Soll. Duvash. House 
Aldenmere. And now this. You are not an accident, Pip. You never were. Tonight, you hold a grounding circle 
beside the finest wizards I have ever known, and you will hold it because that is who you have always been — 
someone who shows up when it matters.

Orindel — you gave your street children list to Edric in case you didn't come back. That is the kind of person 
you are. You think about the ones who have no one else. Tonight, you stand in that chamber and you keep four 
people alive through eleven minutes of fire. There is no one I trust more to do it.

Veyra — you have not known us long. But you are walking into that chamber to face someone you loved for sixty 
years, someone who has become something you do not recognize, and you are doing it because it is right. Not 
because we asked. Because you know that if anyone can still reach Thessara Kain, it is you. That is not bravery. 
That is grace.

And I —"

He pauses. Touches the pocket where the signet ring sits.

"I am a drow paladin with a ruined ring and a borrowed sword, leading a company of rogues, clerics, wizards, and 
one halfling who set a fence post on fire and called it a triumph."

Pip makes a sound that is half laugh, half sob.

"I do not know what waits for us below that mountain. I do not know if we will all walk out. But I know this — 
what we do tonight is the most noble thing any of us will ever do. We are not doing it for glory. We are not 
doing it for gold. We are doing it because four thousand people are sleeping above us right now, and they deserve
to wake up tomorrow.

They will never know our names. They will never know this night happened. And that is exactly as it should be. 
Because this is what courage looks like — not the kind that gets written into songs, but the kind that walks into
the dark because someone has to.

You are the finest people I have ever known. Every one of you. And I am honored — honored — to walk into this with
you beside me.

Now. Let us go and save this city." --Zaryth
