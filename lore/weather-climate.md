# Weather & Climate — The Hearthcoast

> Regional weather patterns by location and season. Used for travel planning, encounter design, and homestead management.
> **Hook:** Reference when determining travel conditions, sea state, crop timing, or environmental hazards.

---

## Climate Overview

The Hearthcoast is a **temperate maritime** region — mild winters, cool summers, frequent rain, persistent fog in low-lying areas. The Greypeak Mountains to the north create a rain shadow effect: the western coast and marshes are wetter, the eastern interior is drier.

| Zone | Hex Range | Climate Type | Key Feature |
|------|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Fenhollow Marshes** | G8–H8 | Maritime marsh | Fog, high humidity, mild temps, frequent rain. Frost rare except Deepwinter. |
| **Hearthcoast (central)** | I9–K12 | Maritime coastal | Sea breeze, moderate rain, mild year-round. Best sailing weather. |
| **Greypeak Mountains** | D1–E5 | Alpine/subalpine | Cold, snow above 3,000 ft year-round. Harsh winters. Short summers. |
| **Thornveil Forest** | F6–G7 | Temperate forest | Sheltered, humid, heavy canopy traps moisture. Cool even in summer. |
| **Western Coast (Stormcape)** | A5–A10 | Exposed maritime | Extreme wind, salt spray, storms. Mild temps but brutal weather. |
| **Southern Sea (Veldmere)** | K14–L16 | Warm maritime | Warmer than north. Longer growing season. Calmer seas (summer). |
| **Northern Highlands** | D1–E2 | Subarctic scrubland | Barren, wind-scoured, cold year-round. Beyond the ward line. |
| **Underdark (Deep Road)** | Below surface | Constant | 13°C year-round. 90% humidity. No weather. No seasons. |

## Underdark Climate

The Deep Road and Underdark have no weather and no seasons. Conditions are constant:

| Factor | Value | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Temperature | 13°C (±3°C) | Warmer near volcanic zones (21 to 38°C). Cooler near surface entrances. |
| Humidity | 85–95% | Near-constant. Condensation on cold surfaces. |
| Air movement | Slow convection currents | Warm air rises through chimneys/sinkholes. Detectable by experienced navigators. |
| Light | None (total darkness) | Bioluminescence in fungal zones. Faerzress glow in some areas. |
| Precipitation | None | Underground rivers and dripping ceilings provide water. |
| Seasonal variation | None | The Underdark doesn't know what month it is. |
| **Exception: Tidal effects** | Full moon (15th) | Underground water levels rise slightly. The Wet Section on the Deep Road floods more during full moons. |
