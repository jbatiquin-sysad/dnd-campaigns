### Founding — Ashara Vel'quinar

Ashara was a scholar, not a warrior. Approximately 400 years ago, she discovered a **Binding-era keeper station** in the deep tunnels beneath the Drow city of Vel'arath — a chamber with an octagonal stone column, Binding script on the walls, and two signet rings of Binding-era alloy on a stone table.

She didn't report the discovery. She studied it in secret, learning that the column connected to a vast underground network — the same Binding infrastructure the Dawnwatch later rediscovered. She couldn't activate it (she lacked the attunement), but she understood its significance.

She founded House Vel'quinar to protect this knowledge. The house sigil — spider over crescent moon — encoded the house's dual identity: Drow by heritage, Eilistraee by faith. Ashara believed Eilistraee was the deity closest to the Binding's resonance — moonlight and frequency share harmonic properties.

### The Rings

The two signet rings Ashara found are **Binding-era alloy** — the same pre-Sundering metal as the ward instruments in the Fenhollow and Aelindra keeper station vaults. They were not forged by Drow. They were forged by the Binders — the civilization that created the ward network.

The rings were passed down through the house: one for the Matron, one for the heir.

- **Ilyana's ring:** Intact. Whole. Carried for decades.
- **Zaryth's ring:** Broken deliberately by Ilyana during the escape (~1241 AS) to disguise it as a worthless relic. A whole Binding-era ring would attract attention from anyone who recognized the alloy.

Both rings respond to the Binding frequency. They are warm to the touch for attuned carriers. Zaryth's broken ring reacted to the Old Foundation glyph (Day 223, 1248 AS) — faerzress glow, vibration.

| 3rd | Syndra | ~920 AS | First to attempt activation of the station column. Failed — no attunement. |

**The core revelation:** The Drow — all elves — are descended from the Binders. The elves who went underground after the Sundering carried Binding-era knowledge with them. Over centuries, it was lost under Lolth's worship, house wars, and survival. House Vel'quinar preserved a fragment of it for 400 years. Zaryth is the first in the line to actually *use* it.

### Zaryth's Escape & Memory Gap

**~1241 AS.** House Vel'sharen attacked Vel'quinar at dawn. 200 warriors vs 40. Kael (Weapons Master, Ilyana's brother) held the rear guard — took a blade meant for Ilyana. Ilyana evacuated 12 survivors into the volcanic tunnels.

Zaryth was sent on a separate route — upward, toward the surface. The route passed through **Ashara's keeper station**. Ilyana suppressed Zaryth's memory of the route using an Eilistraee ritual ("Eilistraee's Mercy") — a divine spell that blocks specific memories without damaging the mind. Purpose: if Zaryth was captured, he couldn't reveal the route to the enclave or the station.

**The memory can be restored.** Priestess Sera can reverse the ritual. Requirements: moonlight, Zaryth's consent. The memories will return — the route, the escape, everything he saw. Some of it may be difficult.

**Zaryth's fragments:** Running through tunnels. Stone dust. A light that wasn't bioluminescence. Wind on his face for the first time. These are real memories from the edges of the suppression — the ritual blocked the route details but not the sensory experience.

## The City of Vel'arath

### Overview

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Name** | Vel'arath ("Shadow of the Veil" in old Elvish) |
| **Location** | Deep Underdark, south-southeast of the Greypeaks. Est. surface hex E6–F6, depth -3 to -5 miles. |
| **Population (pre-fall)** | ~8,000 Drow + ~3,000 slaves |
| **Ruling Council** | 20 ranked houses. First House: Vel'sharen. |
| **Cavern** | Natural limestone cathedral, ~2 miles across, 800 ft ceiling. Stalactite towers (noble houses), stalagmite fortresses (military), web bridges, fungal farms, slave quarters. |
| **Access from Deep Road** | Via the Y Fork right branch → volcanic tunnels → deeper passages → Vel'arath. Estimated 40–60 miles from the Y Fork. 5–8 days on foot. |
| **Current status** | **UNKNOWN.** House Vel'sharen controls the city. No contact since the fall of Vel'quinar (8 years ago). |

### Geography

Vel'arath sits in a massive natural cavern in the middle Underdark — the layer where Drow cities are built (3–10 miles deep). The cavern was formed by an ancient underground river that has since shifted course, leaving a dry cathedral of limestone with a vaulted ceiling.

The city is built vertically:
- **Ceiling (stalactites):** Noble houses. Higher = higher rank. House Vel'sharen occupies the highest stalactite cluster.
- **Mid-level (web bridges):** Markets, temples, academies. The Bazaar is neutral ground.
- **Floor (stalagmites):** Military installations, slave quarters, fungal farms.
- **Below the floor:** Mining tunnels, waste disposal, and — unknown to most — **Ashara's keeper station.**

### Ashara's Keeper Station (Third Station)

The keeper station Ashara discovered is beneath the city of Vel'arath. It is the **third known Binding-era anchor station** (after Fenhollow and Aelindra). Its status is unknown — it may be intact (like Fenhollow, sealed for centuries) or damaged (like Aelindra, earthquake-compromised).

| Detail | Known | Unknown |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Location | Beneath Vel'arath, deep tunnels | Exact depth, exact access route |
| Condition | Ashara studied it ~400 years ago | Current state (8 years since last Vel'quinar contact) |
| Column | Present (octagonal, Binding script) | Reading (% active?) |
| Pedestals | Present (8) | Empty or occupied? |
| Vault | Unknown | May contain materials like Fenhollow/Aelindra vaults |
| Access | Through tunnels below the city | Route known only to Vel'quinar (memory-suppressed in Zaryth) |

**Implication:** If this station can be reached and activated, it would be the third anchor in the network. Combined with Fenhollow (91%) and Aelindra (58%), three of nine anchor stations would be online — dramatically strengthening the ward network.

**Risk:** The station is beneath a hostile Drow city controlled by House Vel'sharen. Reaching it means either infiltrating Vel'arath or finding an alternate route through the deep tunnels.

## The Grey-Cloaked Drow — IDENTIFIED

The Drow woman spotted at Veldmere docks (late 1248 AS) asking about Dawnwatch goods:

- Female, mid-30s
- Grey cloak, arrived alone by ship from southern routes
- Asked about the Dawnwatch supply chain (commercial questions, not tactical)
- Sigil on cloak clasp: spider over crescent moon (Vel'quinar sigil) — **silver** (outer circle designation)
- Serevane's assessment: wants to be found
- Revka's assessment: not part of regular Drow merchant group

**IDENTIFIED by Matron Ilyana (Day 131, 1249 AS): Nhilara Vel'quinar.** Outer circle member — the house's surface trade liaison, operating in the southern ports (Veldmere and further south) for over a decade. She managed Vel'quinar's legitimate surface commerce under a cover identity. She was not in Vel'arath when the house fell.

Nhilara has been searching for Vel'quinar survivors for **8 years**. She didn't know where to look until the Dawnwatch name spread across the Hearthcoast. The Heartstone activation and the Reaping Fair victories gave her a trail.

**Status:** Revka (Veldmere) and Serevane (Duskport) instructed to locate and direct her to Fenhollow. She is family. She is also the most exposed Vel'quinar member — operating alone on the surface with a visible house sigil. Vel'sharen surface agents may be tracking her.

**Threat:** If Vel'sharen agents identify Nhilara as Vel'quinar before the Dawnwatch finds her, they could follow her to Fenhollow — or eliminate her.
