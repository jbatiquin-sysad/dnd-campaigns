# Drow Society

## Overview

The Drow (Dark Elves) are a subrace of elves who dwell in the Underdark — vast subterranean cavern networks miles beneath the surface. They are characterized by obsidian-black skin, white or silver hair, and eyes that range from red to lavender to pale blue. They possess superior darkvision (120 ft) but suffer in direct sunlight (Sunlight Sensitivity).

Drow society is ruthless, hierarchical, and matriarchal. Power is the only currency that matters. Betrayal is not a crime — it is a career strategy. The weak serve the strong, and the strong serve the Spider Queen.

---

## Religion — Lolth, the Spider Queen

Lolth (also called the Queen of Spiders, the Dark Mother) is the primary deity of the Drow. Her worship dominates nearly every aspect of Drow civilization.

**Core tenets:**
- The strong rule. The weak serve or die.
- Chaos is strength. Predictability is weakness.
- Females are superior. Males exist to serve.
- Betrayal in Lolth's name is holy. Loyalty is a tool, not a virtue.
- Spiders are sacred. Killing a spider is blasphemy.

**Priestesses (Yochlol's Chosen):** Lolth's clergy are exclusively female. Priestesses wield divine magic and hold the highest social rank in Drow society — above even the Matron Mothers in spiritual authority. A high priestess can command armies, condemn nobles, and reshape house politics with a word.

**Driders:** Drow who fail Lolth's tests are transformed into driders — half-drow, half-spider monstrosities. They are exiled from cities and live in the wild tunnels. Being turned into a drider is the ultimate punishment and shame.

**Dissidents:** Not all Drow worship Lolth. A minority follow Eilistraee (goddess of beauty, song, and moonlight — good-aligned Drow), Vhaeraun (god of thievery and male Drow rights), or no deity at all. These Drow are hunted, exiled, or killed. Zaryth's rejection of corrupt authority aligns with this dissident tradition, though he follows no Drow deity.

## House Structure

Drow civilization is organized into **Houses** — noble families that function as political factions, military units, and economic powers simultaneously.

### House Hierarchy

```
MATRON MOTHER (absolute ruler of the house)
  │
  ├── First Priestess (eldest daughter, heir apparent)
  ├── High Priestesses (other daughters, clergy)
  │
  ├── House Wizard (male, arcane advisor — high status for a male)
  ├── Weapons Master (male, military commander — highest male rank)
  │
  ├── House Captain(s) (male officers, patrol/garrison leaders)
  ├── House Warriors (male soldiers, guards)
  │
  ├── Commoners (crafters, merchants, servants)
  │
  └── Slaves (captured surface dwellers, goblinoids, other Drow)
```

### House Rankings

Every Drow city maintains a **Ruling Council** of the most powerful houses, ranked by strength. The First House rules the city. Rankings shift through:
- **House Wars** — open military conflict (legal if no survivors from the losing house can testify)
- **Assassination** — of key leaders (legal if unproven)
- **Political maneuvering** — alliances, betrayals, economic warfare
- **Lolth's favor** — demonstrated through priestess power, successful raids, and spider omens

**The cardinal rule of house warfare:** *It didn't happen if no one can prove it.* A house can be utterly destroyed overnight, and if the attacking house leaves no witnesses, the Ruling Council will simply adjust the rankings. If witnesses survive, the attacking house is punished — not for the attack, but for the incompetence of being caught.

### Drow Historiography (Weaponized History)

Drow historical records are political tools, not neutral archives. Houses and priestesses routinely revise, delete, or fabricate history to preserve status and control narratives.

- Fallen houses are often erased from official records within a few generations.
- Disfavored deities, dissidents, and traitors are written out of temple chronicles.
- Contradictory accounts may coexist by design, letting ruling houses declare whichever version is "truth."
- "Official history" in one city may directly conflict with another city's records.

**DM use:** Treat drow historical claims as biased sources. Conflicting accounts are not mistakes; they are evidence of active political censorship.

### House Vel'quinar (Zaryth's House)

Status unknown. The broken signet ring may indicate:
- The house was destroyed (ring broken by an enemy)
- Zaryth was formally exiled (ring broken by the house as a severing ritual)
- Zaryth broke it himself (rejection of the house's authority)

Any of these would make Zaryth an outcast in Drow society — houseless, clanless, with no legal standing. In the Underdark, a houseless Drow is either prey or a mercenary.

## Gender Roles

Drow society is strictly matriarchal:

| Role | Gender | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| Matron Mother | Female | Absolute ruler of a house |
| Priestess | Female only | Lolth grants divine magic only to females |
| Noble | Female (primary) | Daughters inherit, sons serve |
| Wizard | Male (typically) | Arcane magic is the male path to power |
| Weapons Master | Male | Highest military rank a male can hold |
| Warrior/Soldier | Male (mostly) | Expendable front-line fighters |
| Merchant/Crafter | Either | Lower status regardless of gender |
| Slave | Either | No rights, no status |

Males who show ambition are watched carefully. A male who becomes too powerful, too popular, or too independent is eliminated — unless he is useful enough to a priestess to be protected. Zaryth's career as a decorated officer on the surface is extraordinary for a male Drow and would be viewed with deep suspicion by traditional Drow society.

## Daily Life

### Cities
Drow cities are built in enormous natural caverns, often miles across. Architecture features:
- Stalactite towers (hanging from cavern ceilings)
- Stalagmite fortresses (rising from cavern floors)
- Web bridges (spider-silk, enchanted for strength)
- Bioluminescent lighting (fungal, magical, or faerzress-powered)
- Slave quarters (lowest levels, worst conditions)

### Economy
- **Slave trade** — the backbone of Drow economy. Raids on surface settlements and other Underdark races provide labor.
- **Poison** — Drow sleep poison is a major export. Crafted from fungi and spider venom.
- **Mushroom farming** — staple food. Varieties range from bland nutrition to rare delicacies.
- **Adamantine** — Drow smiths work adamantine ore found in deep veins. Adamantine weapons and armor are prized.
- **Spider silk** — stronger than steel by weight. Used for armor, rope, clothing, and trade goods.
- **Magic items** — Drow enchanters produce items that often lose their magic in sunlight (a deliberate design to prevent surface use).

### Education
- Females: trained at the temple (priestess academy). Combat, divine magic, politics, poison craft.
- Males: trained at the warrior academy or the arcane academy. Combat, tactics, arcane magic, survival.
- All Drow learn: Undercommon, Elvish (drow dialect), Common (for trade/raids), Drow Sign Language (silent tactical communication), basic poison identification, dagger fighting, and the social rules of survival.

## Values & Psychology

| Surface Value | Drow Equivalent |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Trust | Calculated risk |
| Loyalty | Useful until it isn't |
| Honor | Reputation (fear-based) |
| Love | Possession / alliance |
| Mercy | Weakness (unless strategic) |
| Justice | Power exercised |
| Freedom | The ability to dominate others |

**Paranoia is survival.** Every Drow assumes everyone around them is plotting. They are usually correct. Sleep is taken in shifts, food is tested for poison, and alliances are temporary by definition.

**Exceptions exist.** Not every Drow is evil. Dissidents, exiles, and those who have lived on the surface long enough to learn different values can break the pattern. Zaryth is proof of this — but in the Underdark, he will be judged by Drow standards, not surface ones.
