# Drow Stat Blocks

> **Usage:** DM combat reference. All stat blocks are homebrew based on Forgotten Realms conventions, balanced for D&D 5e.

## Drow Warrior (CR ¼)

*Medium Humanoid (Elf), Neutral Evil*

**AC** 15 (chain shirt) | **HP** 13 (3d8) | **Speed** 30 ft

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 10 | 14 | 10 | 11 | 11 | 12 |

**Skills** Perception +2, Stealth +4
**Senses** Darkvision 120 ft, Passive Perception 12
**Languages** Common, Elvish, Undercommon, Drow Sign Language
**CR** ¼ (50 XP)

**Fey Ancestry.** Advantage on saves vs. charmed, immune to magical sleep.
**Sunlight Sensitivity.** Disadvantage on attacks and Perception in direct sunlight.
**Innate Spellcasting (CHA, DC 11).** At will: Dancing Lights. 1/day each: Faerie Fire, Darkness.

**Actions:**
- **Shortsword.** +4 to hit, reach 5 ft, 5 (1d6+2) piercing.
- **Hand Crossbow.** +4 to hit, range 30/120, 5 (1d6+2) piercing + Drow Poison (DC 13 CON or poisoned 1 hr; fail by 5+ = unconscious 1 hr).

*Medium Humanoid (Elf), Neutral Evil*

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |

- **Multiattack.** Three Shadow Sword attacks.

This kills any wizard in the party outright. Use ONLY as a major story antagonist. The party should learn about the Shadowblade before encountering it — fear is the weapon.

## Quick Reference — Encounter Building

**XP thresholds for 5 level-3 PCs (2014 DMG):** Easy 375, Medium 750, Hard 1,125, Deadly 2,000 adjusted XP.

| Encounter | Composition | Base XP | Adjusted XP* | Difficulty (5 x Lv3) |
|-----------|-------------|---------|--------------|----------------------|
| Scout pair | 2 Warriors (flee if spotted) | 100 | 150 | Easy |
| Small patrol | 2 Warriors | 100 | 150 | Easy |
| Pressure patrol | 4 Warriors | 200 | 400 | Easy/Medium (terrain dependent) |
| Strike team | 2 Warriors + 1 Elite | 1,900 | 3,800 | Deadly+ |
| Tunnel patrol (full) | 4 Warriors + 1 Elite | 2,000 | 4,000 | Over-Deadly |
| Ambush cell | 3 Warriors + 1 Elite | 1,950 | 3,900 | Over-Deadly |
| Raiding party | 6 Warriors + 2 Elites + Priestess | 7,800 | 19,500 | **TPK — flee or negotiate** |
| Lone drider | 1 Drider (SRD) | 2,300 | 2,300 | Deadly |
| Drider + slaves | 1 Drider + 4 Grimlocks | 2,700 | 5,400 | Over-Deadly |

\*Adjusted XP uses standard multipliers by monster count.

### Balance Dials (important after adding Warrior Faerie Fire/Darkness)

- Treat routine patrols as having **already spent one 1/day innate spell** unless alerted.
- Do not have every warrior cast Darkness in round 1. Use 1 caster per skirmish cell.
- Prefer **2-4 warrior encounters** for level-3 attrition play; reserve elites for set pieces.
- If you run an elite-led patrol, telegraph it early and include retreat routes.

### Fail-Forward Outcomes (When the Party Loses)

Instead of defaulting to a total-party kill, use outcomes that preserve danger and create new objectives:

| Defeat State | Default Drow Choice | Campaign Consequence |
|--------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| 1-2 PCs down, party retreats | Pursuit and shadowing, not full chase | Alert level rises; next route is trapped |
| Party wiped in skirmish with warriors | Capture for sale/interrogation | Escape arc, gear recovery, forced alliance options |
| Party defeated by elite/priestess cell | Targeted prisoner extraction (take 1 key PC) | Split-party rescue mission under time pressure |
| Party wins but noisily | Drow withdraw and report | House bounty issued, prices rise in Underdark markets |

**Hard rule for tone:** Drow prefer value extraction (slaves, leverage, information) over battlefield slaughter unless under direct house orders.

**For a Level 3 party of 5:** Organized drow forces are intentionally beyond fair-fight difficulty. The intended play pattern is stealth, misdirection, social leverage, and selective strikes, not stand-up battle.
