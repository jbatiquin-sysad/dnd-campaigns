# Drow Military & Tactics

## Military Structure

Every Drow house maintains a standing military force. Size scales with house rank:

| House Rank | Typical Force | Leadership |
|------------|--------------|------------|
| First House | 500–1,000 warriors + slaves | Multiple Weapons Masters, dozens of captains |
| Top 5 Houses | 200–500 warriors | 1–2 Weapons Masters, 10+ captains |
| Minor Houses | 50–200 warriors | 1 Weapons Master, 3–5 captains |
| Fallen/Exiled | Scattered survivors, mercenaries | None formal |

### Ranks

| Rank | Role | Gender |
|------|------|--------|
| Priestess of Lolth | Spiritual and strategic command authority; often commands major patrols/raids | Female |
| Weapons Master | Supreme military commander of a house | Male |
| House Captain | Company commander, patrol leader, garrison chief | Usually male, sometimes female |
| Elite Warrior | Veteran, specialist, bodyguard | Any (often male) |
| Warrior | Standard soldier | Any (often male) |
| Slave Soldier | Expendable front line (goblinoids, bugbears, ogres) | Any |

## Patrol Structure

Drow patrols operate in the tunnels surrounding their cities. Standard compositions:
Most patrols are mixed-sex. Larger and elite patrols are commonly commanded by a female priestess, often with elite bodyguards and junior clergy.

### Tunnel Patrol (Standard)
- 4× Drow Warriors
- 1× Drow Elite Warrior (patrol leader)
- Purpose: territory enforcement, intruder detection, slave capture
- Range: 1–5 miles from city

### Raiding Party
- 8–12× Drow Warriors
- 2× Drow Elite Warriors
- 1× Drow Priestess (if targeting a significant objective)
- 4–8× Slave Soldiers (bugbears, ogres, or grimlocks)
- Purpose: surface raids, rival house strikes, slave acquisition
- Range: up to 50 miles, multi-day operations

### House War Column
- 50–200× Drow Warriors
- 10–20× Drow Elite Warriors
- 2–4× House Captains
- 1× Weapons Master
- 1–3× Priestesses
- 1× House Wizard (if available)
- Slave auxiliaries (variable)
- Driders (if the house controls any — used as shock troops)
- Purpose: house-on-house warfare, city defense

## Combat Tactics

### Doctrine: "Strike from Darkness"

Drow fight dirty. Their tactical philosophy:

1. **Never fight fair.** Ambush, poison, traps, and superior numbers are the baseline, not the exception.
2. **Control the light.** Cast Darkness on the enemy to break line of sight, split formations, and cover movement. Drow Darkvision does not pierce magical darkness, so they pair it with prepared terrain, practiced coordination, and Faerie Fire.
3. **Poison first, blade second.** Open with sleep-poison crossbow bolts at range. Incapacitate targets before melee.
4. **Capture, don't kill (usually).** Living prisoners have value as slaves, hostages, or interrogation subjects. Dead enemies are worth nothing.
5. **Retreat is not cowardice.** Drow will withdraw, regroup, and attack again from a better position. Chasing a retreating Drow patrol into the tunnels is how surface parties die.

### Standard Engagement Sequence

```
1. Scout detects intruders (Stealth +4 to +7)
2. Patrol positions in ambush (elevated, flanking, behind cover)
3. Priestess or Elite casts Darkness on the enemy group
4. Volley of sleep-poison crossbow bolts (surprise round if undetected)
5. Assess results — if 2+ targets down, close to melee and capture
6. If targets resist, second Darkness + Faerie Fire on resistant targets
7. If losing, retreat into prepared tunnel (caltrops, web traps, collapse points)
8. Report to house command. Reinforcements arrive within hours.
```

### Alert & Response Clock (Campaign Pressure Tool)

Use this when the party repeatedly clashes with drow forces. It keeps stakes high without forcing every scene into a boss fight.

| Alert Level | Trigger Examples | Drow Response |
|-------------|------------------|---------------|
| 0 — Unaware | No witnesses, no surviving scouts | Normal patrols only |
| 1 — Suspicious | Missing patrol, strange tracks, dead slave handlers | More scouts, trap checks, tighter checkpoints |
| 2 — Confirmed Hostiles | Survivors report party description | Elite-led strike teams, standing ambushes, bounty notices |
| 3 — Strategic Threat | Repeated losses or sabotage by party | Priestess-led hunt, diviners, pressure on allied NPCs |
| 4 — House Priority | Party disrupts house assets or kills key officer | Multi-cell sweeps, interdiction of exits, political retaliation |

**Decay rule:** Reduce alert by 1 after 3 quiet in-game days with no contact.

### Counter-Tactics (for the Dawnwatch)

| Drow Tactic | Counter |
|-------------|---------|
| Darkness spell | Drow don't see through magical darkness by default. Use forced movement, readied actions at choke points, and Daylight to break their cover plan. |
| Sleep poison bolts | Enhanced antitoxin (advantage on CON save). Elves have Fey Ancestry (advantage vs. charm/sleep — but Drow poison is CON-based, not magical sleep). Stay in cover. |
| Ambush from elevation | Mira's Perception (expertise, +7). Orindel's Perception (+4). Scout ahead. |
| Retreat into tunnels | Don't chase. Hold position. They'll come back — be ready. |
| Capture attempts | Don't get grappled. Mira's Cunning Action Disengage. Seren's 40 ft speed. |
| Faerie Fire on party | Dispel Magic (Sylvara/Bram). Or accept it and fight through — it lasts 1 minute. |

### Drow Sleep Poison

**Type:** Injury (applied to weapons, typically crossbow bolts)
**Save:** CON DC 13
**Effect:** Poisoned for 1 hour. If the save fails by 5 or more, the target is also unconscious for 1 hour or until it takes damage or another creature uses an action to shake it awake.
**Craft:** Requires Underdark fungi + spider venom. Drow alchemists produce it in bulk. Loses potency after 24 hours in sunlight.
**Value:** 50–100 gp per dose on the surface (rare, illegal in most cities). Common in the Underdark.

### Drow Adamantine Equipment

Adamantine is found in deep Underdark veins. Drow smiths are among the few who work it:
- **Adamantine armor:** Critical hits against the wearer become normal hits.
- **Adamantine weapons:** Automatically score critical hits against objects. Bypass hardness.
- **Rarity:** Rare. Only elite warriors and house leaders carry adamantine gear.

### Sunlight Degradation

Many Drow-crafted magic items lose their enchantment after prolonged exposure to sunlight (typically 1–2 hours of direct sun). This is sometimes intentional (to prevent surface races from using captured Drow gear) and sometimes a side effect of Underdark enchanting techniques that rely on faerzress (ambient Underdark magical radiation).

## Faerzress

Faerzress is ambient magical radiation found throughout the Underdark. It is strongest near Drow cities and in certain geological formations.

**Effects:**
- Interferes with teleportation and divination magic (unreliable or blocked entirely)
- Powers some Drow enchantments and wards
- Can cause wild magic surges in areas of high concentration
- Provides faint illumination in some caverns (blue-purple glow)

**Tactical implication:** Teleportation-based escape plans (Misty Step, Dimension Door) may fail in faerzress-heavy areas. Scrying and Sending may be unreliable. The party should not rely on magical communication or extraction in the deep Underdark.
