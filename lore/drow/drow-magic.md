# Drow Magic

## Innate Drow Magic

All Drow possess innate spellcasting (Charisma-based), a gift of their elven heritage twisted by centuries in the Underdark:

| Level | Ability | Frequency |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| 1st | **Dancing Lights** (cantrip) | At will |
| 3rd | **Faerie Fire** | 1/day |
| 5th | **Darkness** | 1/day |

These abilities require no material components. They function normally in the Underdark but are suppressed in areas of anti-magic.

**Tactical note:** Faerie Fire and Darkness are the backbone of Drow combat tactics. Faerie Fire outlines targets (advantage on attacks, negates invisibility). Darkness creates a 15-foot-radius sphere of magical darkness that even Darkvision cannot penetrate. Drow do not automatically see through it; they use terrain familiarity, practiced signals, and preplanned kill zones.

## Priestess Magic (Divine — Lolth)

Drow priestesses are clerics of Lolth. They wield divine magic with a focus on domination, darkness, and spider themes.

### Common Priestess Spells

| Level | Spells | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Cantrips | Thaumaturgy, Poison Spray, Guidance | |
| 1st | Command, Inflict Wounds, Shield of Faith, Bane | |
| 2nd | Hold Person, Silence, Spiritual Weapon, Web* | *Web is arcane but granted by Lolth |
| 3rd | Bestow Curse, Dispel Magic, Spirit Guardians | |
| 4th | Banishment, Freedom of Movement, Guardian of Faith | |
| 5th | Contagion, Insect Plague (spiders), Flame Strike | |

### Signature Priestess Abilities

- **Summon Spider:** Priestesses of 5th level+ can summon Giant Spiders (1–4 depending on level). Concentration, 1 hour.
- **Lolth's Blessing:** 1/day, a priestess can impose disadvantage on one saving throw against her spell. Represents Lolth's direct intervention.
- **Spider Walk:** Priestesses of 7th level+ can walk on walls and ceilings (as Spider Climb, at will, self only).

## Arcane Magic (Wizard Tradition)

Male Drow who show arcane talent are trained at the arcane academy. Drow wizards favor:

### Preferred Schools
- **Evocation** — direct damage, tunnel warfare
- **Illusion** — deception, ambush enhancement
- **Conjuration** — summoning, battlefield control
- **Abjuration** — wards, house defense

### Common Drow Wizard Spells

| Level | Spells | Tactical Use |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| Cantrips | Fire Bolt, Ray of Frost, Minor Illusion, Mage Hand | |
| 1st | Shield, Magic Missile, Sleep, Detect Magic | Shield is critical — Drow wizards are fragile |
| 2nd | Web, Mirror Image, Misty Step, Invisibility | Web + Darkness = trapped and blind enemies |
| 3rd | Fireball, Counterspell, Dispel Magic, Fly | Fireball in tunnels is devastating (and indiscriminate) |
| 4th | Greater Invisibility, Dimension Door, Evard's Black Tentacles | |

### Drow Wizard Limitations
- Male wizards hold high status *for males* but are still subordinate to priestesses
- A wizard who outshines a priestess politically will be eliminated
- Wizards serve as tactical advisors, not commanders
- Some wizards secretly worship Vhaeraun (god of male Drow) — this is heresy and punishable by transformation into a drider

## Faerzress & Anti-Divination

The Underdark's ambient magical radiation (faerzress) has significant effects on spellcasting:

| Spell Type | Effect in Faerzress |
|------------|-------------------|
| Teleportation (Misty Step, Dimension Door, Teleport) | Unreliable — 50% chance of failure or random destination |
| Divination (Scrying, Locate Object, Detect Thoughts) | Blocked or returns false information |
| Sending | Works but may be intercepted or garbled |
| Conjuration (summoning) | Functions normally |
| Evocation, Abjuration, Illusion | Functions normally |
| Wild Magic | Faerzress-heavy areas may trigger wild magic surges (DM discretion) |

**Campaign implication:** The Dawnwatch cannot rely on Sending Stones to contact Vaelith from deep in the Underdark. Scrying won't find them. Teleportation escape plans are unreliable. They go in, they come out the way they went in — or they find another exit.
