# The Underdark — Drow Perspective

## Geography

The Underdark is a vast network of subterranean caverns, tunnels, and bodies of water extending miles beneath the surface of the world. It is divided into three layers:

### The Three Layers

| Layer | Depth | Character | Inhabitants |
|-------|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **Upper Underdark** | Surface – 3 miles | Natural caves, mines, sinkholes. Connected to surface. Some light from above. Relatively navigable. | Goblins, kobolds, grimlocks, troglodytes, surface exiles, outlaws, shallow-dwelling monsters |
| **Middle Underdark** | 3 – 10 miles | Vast caverns (miles across), fungal forests, underground rivers and lakes. Drow cities. Total darkness. Faerzress radiation. | Drow, duergar (grey dwarves), deep gnomes (svirfneblin), mind flayers, beholders, hook horrors, ropers |
| **Deep Underdark** | 10+ miles | Alien geology. Extreme pressure and heat. Underground seas. Reality thins. | Aboleths, elder things, purple worms, entities that predate the surface races. Even Drow avoid this layer. |

### Navigation

The Underdark has no sun, no stars, no compass directions. Navigation relies on:

- **Tunnel memory** — experienced Underdark travelers memorize routes by feel, echo, and air current
- **Resonance markers** — carved stones that hum at specific frequencies (Vigil and Drow both use variants)
- **Bioluminescence patterns** — certain fungi grow in predictable patterns near water, heat, or faerzress
- **Air currents** — moving air means a connection to a larger space or the surface
- **Sound** — echoes reveal cavern size, water presence, and creature movement
- **Faerzress glow** — faint blue-purple radiation marks certain passages and cavern walls

**Zaryth's skills:** As a Drow raised in the Underdark, Zaryth retains navigation instincts. He can read air currents, identify fungal markers, and interpret resonance carvings. This is the party's primary advantage — without a guide, surface dwellers are lost within hours.

## Drow Cities

Drow cities are built in enormous natural caverns, typically 1–5 miles across with ceilings hundreds of feet high. Major features:

### Architecture

- **Stalactite towers** — noble houses build downward from the cavern ceiling. Higher (closer to the ceiling) = higher status.
- **Stalagmite fortresses** — military installations and slave quarters built upward from the floor.
- **Web bridges** — spider-silk suspension bridges connecting stalactite and stalagmite structures. Enchanted for strength.
- **The Bazaar** — open market on the cavern floor. Merchants, slavers, mercenaries, information brokers. Neutral ground (violence in the Bazaar is punished by all houses collectively).
- **Temple of Lolth** — always the largest structure. Central to the city. Priestess academy attached.
- **Arcane Academy** — male wizard training. Usually built into a stalagmite near the Bazaar.

### Notable Drow Cities (Forgotten Realms)

| City | Location | Character | Notes |
|------|----------|-----------|-------|
| **Menzoberranzan** | Northdark, beneath the Spine of the World | Largest and most powerful. 20,000+ Drow. Ruled by the Council of Eight (top 8 houses). | The "default" Drow city in Forgotten Realms lore. |
| **Ched Nasad** | Middledark, beneath the Far Hills | Built entirely on calcified webs spanning a vast chasm. "City of Shimmering Webs." | Destroyed and partially rebuilt multiple times. |

## Other Underdark Races

The Drow are not alone in the Underdark. Relations with other races:

### Duergar (Grey Dwarves)

- **Alignment:** Lawful Evil
- **Relation to Drow:** Hostile rivals. Compete for territory and resources. Occasional uneasy trade agreements.
- **Traits:** Invisible at will, enlarge at will, resistant to illusions, immune to charm/paralysis. Sunlight Sensitivity.
- **Society:** Militaristic, joyless, slave-based economy. Worship Laduguer (god of toil).
- **Encounter:** Duergar patrols are disciplined and organized. They fight in formation. They don't take prisoners — they take slaves.

### Svirfneblin (Deep Gnomes)

- **Alignment:** Neutral Good
- **Relation to Drow:** Prey. Drow raid svirfneblin settlements for slaves. Deep gnomes hide, flee, and avoid Drow at all costs.
- **Traits:** Superior Darkvision (120 ft), Stone Camouflage (advantage on Stealth in rocky terrain), Nondetection (innate).
- **Society:** Small, hidden communities. Gem miners. Peaceful but paranoid (justifiably).
- **Encounter:** Potential allies if the party can earn trust. They know the tunnels better than anyone.

### Mind Flayers (Illithids)

- **Alignment:** Lawful Evil
- **Relation to Drow:** Mutual hatred and fear. Mind flayers enslave Drow when they can. Drow destroy mind flayer colonies when they find them. Neither side negotiates.
- **Traits:** Telepathy 120 ft, Mind Blast (60-ft cone, INT save or stunned), Extract Brain (instant kill on grappled target), psionic spellcasting.
- **Society:** Hive-mind colonies ruled by an Elder Brain. Breed by implanting tadpoles in humanoid hosts (ceremorphosis).
- **Encounter:** CR 7 per mind flayer. A colony has 20–50. **Do not engage a colony at Level 3.** A single mind flayer is a deadly boss fight. Mind Blast can stun the entire party in one action.

### Aboleth

- **Alignment:** Lawful Evil
- **Relation to Drow:** Ancient and unknowable. Aboleths predate all surface races. They remember everything — including the world before the Sundering. Drow avoid aboleth territory.
- **Traits:** Telepathy 120 ft, Enslave (DC 14 WIS or charmed indefinitely), Mucus Cloud (underwater), perfect memory spanning millennia.
- **Encounter:** CR 10. Negotiation encounter first — an aboleth may trade information for service. It knows things no one else knows. It also lies.

### Myconids (Fungal Folk)

- **Alignment:** Lawful Neutral
- **Relation to Drow:** Ignored or enslaved. Myconids are peaceful and non-threatening. Drow consider them beneath notice.
- **Traits:** Rapport Spores (telepathic communication), Pacifying Spores (CON save or stunned), Animating Spores (raise dead as spore servants).
- **Society:** Communal, peaceful, fungal gardens. Led by a Sovereign.
- **Encounter:** Potential allies and rest stops. Myconid circles are safe havens in the Underdark — if you can communicate (Rapport Spores required).

## Underdark Ecology

### Food Sources

| Source | Location | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Fungi (edible) | Everywhere | Staple food. Dozens of varieties. Some nutritious, some hallucinogenic, some lethal. Survival DC 12 to identify safe varieties. |
| Rothé (Underdark cattle) | Near settlements | Deep rothé are domesticated by Drow and duergar. Meat, leather, bone. |
| Fish (blind cave fish) | Underground rivers/lakes | Bland but nutritious. Easy to catch. |
| Lizards/insects | Everywhere | Protein source. Giant varieties are hunted. |
| Zurkhwood (giant mushroom) | Fungal forests | Wood substitute. Used for construction, fuel, and tools. Grows to 30 ft. |

### Water

Underground rivers and lakes are common. Water is generally safe but may contain:
- Mineral contamination (metallic taste, mild poison in some areas)
- Aboleth mucus (near aboleth territory — CON save or disease)
- Faerzress contamination (mild magical effects — tingling, brief hallucinations)

### Light

There is no natural light in the Underdark below the entrance zone. Light sources:
- Bioluminescent fungi (dim light, blue-green, common)
- Faerzress glow (dim light, blue-purple, in certain areas)
- Fire (torches, lanterns — attracts attention)
- Magical light (Dancing Lights, Light cantrip — also attracts attention)

**Tactical note:** Using light in the Underdark is a trade-off. The party needs it (halflings have no darkvision), but it announces their presence to everything within line of sight — which in a cavern can be hundreds of feet.

## Trade Routes

The Underdark has established trade routes between major settlements, maintained by:
- **Drow merchant houses** — armed caravans with slave porters
- **Duergar traders** — heavily guarded, suspicious, but reliable
- **Independent merchants** — rare, dangerous profession, often svirfneblin or surface exiles
- **Zhentarim / surface criminal networks** — some surface organizations maintain Underdark contacts

Trade goods:
- **Surface → Underdark:** Grain, fruit, surface metals, textiles, surface-crafted weapons, livestock, *information about surface politics*
- **Underdark → Surface:** Adamantine, gems, rare fungi, poisons, spider silk, Underdark-forged weapons, *slaves* (illegal on the surface but traded covertly)
