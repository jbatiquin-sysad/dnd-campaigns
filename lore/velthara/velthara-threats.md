# Vel'thara Threats — Boundary, Dissonance & Embassy Risks

> DM reference. The Vel'thara arc is diplomacy, not dungeon crawl. Threats exist to create tension and stakes — combat is the failure state, not the goal. When in doubt, make it a cultural problem before a combat problem.

---

## Why the Boundary Exists

Three perspectives. All partially true.

### Vaelen's Perspective (the Builder)

Vaelen created the ward network — and the boundary — 3,248 years ago. From his account: the frequency plane was unknown, unpredictable, and potentially dangerous. He sealed it to protect the material world. He believed separation was safety.

*"I heard something on the other side. I didn't know what it was. So I built a wall."*

### Ashara's Perspective (the Binder)

Ashara opposed the boundary. She had crossed over, lived among the Vel'thara, learned their language. She believed the boundary was an act of fear — cutting off an entire civilization because mortals couldn't understand them. She spent 3,000 years trying to undo it.

*"He locked the door because he was afraid of the music."*

### The Truth

Both are partially right. The boundary protects **both sides.**

Without it, the frequency plane and the material plane bleed into each other:
- **Material side:** Objects vibrate apart. Sound becomes visible. Thought becomes physical. Reality destabilizes.
- **Frequency side:** Frequency crystallizes into rigid matter. The Vel'thara lose fluidity, movement, life. Their world hardens and dies.

The controlled aperture at the Anchor Point is the correct solution — **a threshold, not a wall or an open door.** Vaelen's mistake was making it absolute. Ashara's mistake was wanting to tear it down entirely. The Dawnwatch found the middle path.

**DM note:** This is the campaign's central metaphor. Walls vs. doors. Fear vs. recklessness. The answer is always the threshold — controlled contact, mutual respect, boundaries that connect rather than divide.

## What the Storm Weavers Patrol Against

The Storm Weavers (Harmony 11) do not patrol against the mortal world. They guard against something else.

The Vel'thara boundary isn't just between their world and ours — it borders **other frequency planes** too. The spaces between harmonies contain chaotic, destructive frequencies that never resolved into civilization. The Storm Weavers call these **dissonant entities** — beings of chaotic frequency that exist in the gaps between harmonies.

Think of them as frequency predators. They are drawn to organized sound the way sharks are drawn to blood. They consume harmonies, leaving noise. They've been kept out for millennia by the Storm Weavers' constant patrol and the boundary wall's integrity.

**Key implication:** If the boundary weakens on the Vel'thara side, dissonant entities could enter **both** worlds — the frequency plane and the material plane. The ward network doesn't just protect mortals from the Vel'thara. It helps protect the Vel'thara from what lives in the dark between frequencies.

## Dissonance as Threat

When harmonies disagree strongly, the resulting dissonance manifests physically in the Vel'thara world:
- Cracks in crystallized sound
- Distortions in the light-towers
- Areas where frequency breaks down into noise — static, grinding, painful to Vel'thara senses

Prolonged dissonance **attracts dissonant entities.** They sense the break in harmony and move toward it.

This is why the 12 harmonies maintain consensus — not just out of philosophy, but out of survival. **Disagreement is literally dangerous.** A political schism between harmonies could crack the boundary from the inside. The Vel'thara's culture of consensus isn't idealism. It's a defense mechanism.

**DM note:** This creates a natural tension. The Dawnwatch's presence introduces new frequencies — mortal emotion, sequential thought, material-plane vibrations. If the cultural exchange goes well, these new frequencies enrich the Vel'thara world. If it goes badly, they could introduce dissonance. The stakes of diplomacy are physical, not just political.

## What Could Go Wrong at the Embassy

The aperture is stable but not invulnerable. Five risk categories:

| Risk | Trigger | Severity | Response |
|------|---------|----------|----------|
| **Deliberate dissonance** | Someone transmits weaponized sound through the aperture — a discordant frequency designed to harm. | High | The Vel'thara can close the aperture from their side. Accord Trust drops. Diplomatic crisis. |
| **Dissonant entity detection** | A dissonant entity senses the aperture opening — a hole in the boundary is a beacon. | Critical | Storm Weavers respond on their side. Mortal side needs ward defense. See encounter table. |
| **Heartstone disturbance** | The Anchor Point's 7 Heartstones are moved, damaged, or drained. Destabilizes the aperture. | High | Aperture flickers, shrinks, or shifts frequency. Anything in transit is at risk. Immediate repair needed. |
| **Unprepared mortal crossing** | A material being enters the Vel'thara side without frequency acclimation. | Moderate | CON save DC 15 per hour. Failure = 1 level of exhaustion. Sensory overload: sound is visible, light has weight, thought echoes. Eventually fatal if not extracted. |
| **Vel'sharen interference** | Station Four (Vel'arath) wall is cracked. Vel'sharen discover the aperture and attempt to exploit or destroy it. | High | Political + military threat. Nhilara's domain. Could escalate to Underdark conflict. |

### Mortal Crossing — Sensory Overload Rules

A mortal entering the Vel'thara frequency plane without preparation:
- **CON save DC 15** at the end of each hour spent on the other side.
- **Failure:** Gain 1 level of exhaustion. Senses flood — sound becomes visible (synesthesia), light has physical weight, thoughts echo audibly.
- **Success:** Endure for another hour. The experience is overwhelming but survivable.
- **6 levels of exhaustion = death** (standard 5e rules).
- **Mitigation:** Vel'thara can teach frequency acclimation (requires Trust 7+). Reduces DC to 10. A crystallized-frequency talisman from the Crystal Smiths (Harmony 9) grants advantage on the save.

## Dissonant Entity — Frequency Predator

> Use sparingly. This is a campaign-level threat, not a random encounter.

**Frequency Predator** — CR 8 (3,900 XP)
*Large aberration, chaotic evil*

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| **AC** | 16 (natural armor — crystallized static) |
| **HP** | 114 (12d10 + 48) |
| **Speed** | 0 ft, fly 50 ft (hover) |
| **STR** | 8 (−1) |
| **DEX** | 18 (+4) |
| **CON** | 18 (+4) |
| **INT** | 6 (−2) |
| **WIS** | 14 (+2) |
| **CHA** | 3 (−4) |

**Damage Resistances:** Bludgeoning, piercing, slashing from nonmagical attacks
**Damage Immunities:** Psychic, thunder
**Damage Vulnerabilities:** Radiant (ward-light, healing frequencies, and divine magic deal radiant)
**Condition Immunities:** Charmed, frightened, prone, grappled
**Senses:** Blindsight 120 ft (frequency-based, cannot see — hears everything), passive Perception 16
**Languages:** None (communicates in dissonance — painful to hear)

**Dissonant Aura.** Any creature that starts its turn within 15 ft makes a DC 15 CON save or takes 2d6 psychic damage and is deafened until the start of its next turn. Success: half damage, not deafened.

**Frequency Drain.** *Melee attack:* +7 to hit, reach 10 ft. *Hit:* 3d8 + 4 psychic damage. The target's highest-level unexpended spell slot is consumed (if any). If the target has no spell slots, they take an additional 1d8 psychic damage.

**Static Burst (Recharge 5–6).** The predator releases a 30-ft-radius burst of dissonant frequency. All creatures in the area make a DC 15 CON save. *Failure:* 4d8 psychic damage + stunned until end of their next turn. *Success:* Half damage, not stunned.

**Weakness — Harmony.** If three or more creatures within 30 ft are producing music, a hymn, or a sustained harmonic tone (requires an action or bonus action to maintain), the predator has disadvantage on all attack rolls and its Dissonant Aura radius shrinks to 5 ft. **Orindel's Ward-Light Hymn counts.** This is the intended counter — music, not swords.

**Tactics:** The predator targets the loudest organized sound first (spellcasters, musicians, the aperture itself). It tries to silence harmony and feed on frequency. It flees if reduced below 30 HP or if sustained harmony is maintained for 3+ rounds — dissonance cannot survive in a space filled with music.

## DM Guidance — Running the Vel'thara Arc

**The Vel'thara arc should NOT become a combat arc.**

Threats exist to create tension and stakes, not to turn the embassy into a dungeon. The primary challenges should be:

- **Cultural:** Misunderstandings, taboo violations, the difficulty of communicating across fundamentally different modes of existence.
- **Diplomatic:** Building trust, managing expectations, navigating the politics of 12 harmonies with different opinions about mortal contact.
- **Philosophical:** What does it mean to open a door between worlds? Who decides? What are the responsibilities?

**Combat is the failure state.** A dissonant entity incursion means something went wrong — a crack in the boundary, a lapse in defense, a consequence of carelessness. It should feel like a crisis, not a Tuesday.

**The best sessions in this arc** will be the ones where the party solves a problem by singing, telling a story, or giving a gift — not by rolling initiative.

**Pacing:** One encounter table roll per in-game week of active embassy operation, or when narratively appropriate. Not every roll produces an event — on a result of 1–2, it's just atmosphere. Let the quiet sessions breathe.
