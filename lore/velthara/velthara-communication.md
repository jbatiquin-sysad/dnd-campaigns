# Vel'thara — Communication

> **Core rule:** The Vel'thara speak in frequency. Mortals speak in words. The gap between those two things is where every interesting scene lives.

---

## The Translation Pipeline

| Layer | Channel | What the Party Gets | Requires |
|-------|---------|---------------------|----------|
| **Full translation** | Vel'thara → Anchor Point interface → Ashara interprets → Common | Specific meaning, nuance, tone. Full dialogue. | Ashara present at Anchor Point |
| **Ring impressions** | Vel'thara → Dawnwatch rings | Emotional impressions. Warmth = positive, cold = negative. Pulse patterns = basic concepts (3 pulses = greeting, rapid = urgency, slow fade = farewell). | Dawnwatch ring equipped |
| **Ambient sensation** | Vel'thara → environment | Pressure changes, temperature shifts, light patterns near the aperture. Beautiful but incomprehensible. | Proximity to aperture |
| **Music** | Direct — no translation needed | Intent, emotion, meaning carried in melody. The Vel'thara hear music natively. | Instrument or voice + aperture |

**Key dependency:** Ashara is the only fluent translator. If she's unavailable, communication drops to impressions and music only. This is a deliberate campaign tension point — the party's most important diplomatic channel runs through a 3,000-year-old prisoner on probation.

---

## Music as Bridge

The Ward-Light Hymn worked because **music IS the Vel'thara's native language.**

- Any music transmitted through the aperture is understood directly.
- **Orindel** (Hymn Composer) and **Pip** (Flutist) can communicate through performance — the Vel'thara hear intent, emotion, and meaning in melody.
- This is the **one channel that doesn't need Ashara.**
- A skilled performance can convey complex ideas — but the "grammar" is emotional, not logical. You can play *sorrow* or *welcome* or *warning*, not "the supply shipment arrives on Tuesday."

| Communication Type | Best Channel |
|--------------------|-------------|
| Emotional tone, greetings, farewells | Music (Orindel/Pip) |
| Specific information, negotiations | Ashara translation |
| Simple yes/no, urgency signals | Ring impressions |
| Complex philosophical exchange | Ashara + hours of unpacking |

## Running Dialogue in Session

### Step 1 — Sensory First

Describe what the Vel'thara transmit as **sensory experience** before any translation:

> *"A warmth spreads through your ring. The air tastes like copper and starlight. You feel the shape of a question."*

### Step 2 — Translation (if Ashara present)

Ashara interprets:

> *Ashara pauses, then: "They're asking about the orchard. The Bright Weavers want to know — do the apple trees sing when they bloom?"*

### Step 3 — Party Response

| Method | Who | Mechanic | Quality |
|--------|-----|----------|---------|
| **Speak through interface** | Zaryth (Warden) | CHA check (see DC table) | Precise but clinical — like reading a letter aloud |
| **Play music** | Orindel, Pip | Performance check (see DC table) | Emotional and rich — but imprecise for specifics |
| **Transmit through rings** | Anyone | No check — automatic | Emotional impression only. Imprecise. Good for yes/no, gratitude, warning. |
| **Ashara relays** | Ashara | No check | Full translation both ways. Best quality. |

### Without Ashara

The party gets **impressions only**. Run these scenes as atmospheric and emotional — the players should *feel* the communication gap.

> *Your ring pulses — three slow beats, a pause, then a cascade of rapid warmth. Something is excited. Something wants to show you something. But what, exactly, you can't tell.*

## Compressed Information

- A single Vel'thara note can contain what would take **pages** in Common.
- Sylvara can analyze recordings with Detect Magic + Arcana, but it takes **hours to unpack a few seconds** of transmission.
- This is why the Atlas of the Vel'thara took a full week to compile.
- DM note: use this to control information flow. The Vel'thara aren't withholding — the party just can't process it fast enough.

---

## Communication Taboos

| Taboo | Vel'thara Equivalent | Consequence |
|-------|---------------------|-------------|
| **Transmit silence deliberately** | Death threat | Immediate withdrawal. Possible Storm Weaver response. Accord violation. |
| **Transmit dissonance intentionally** | Physical assault | Causes them pain. The Speaker would leave. Diplomatic crisis. |
| **Interrupt mid-transmission** | Slapping someone mid-sentence | Deep offense. The Vel'thara will go silent (not the good kind). Recovery takes time. |

**DM note:** The party doesn't know these taboos yet unless Ashara warns them. Use Ashara's briefings as natural exposition. If a PC accidentally triggers one, it's a great dramatic moment — not a campaign-ender.

## DC Table

### Interface Communication (Zaryth — CHA check)

| DC | Complexity | Example |
|----|-----------|---------|
| 13 | Simple concepts | Greeting, yes/no, gratitude, basic request |
| 15 | Complex ideas | Negotiation terms, detailed questions, explaining mortal customs |
| 17 | Abstract/philosophical | Discussing the nature of the boundary, theological concepts, the base note |

### Musical Communication (Orindel/Pip — Performance check)

| DC | Complexity | Example |
|----|-----------|---------|
| 12 | Emotional expression | Joy, sorrow, welcome, warning, farewell |
| 15 | Specific musical message | "We bring a gift," "Danger approaches," "We remember your name" |
| 18 | Complex narrative in music | Telling a story, conveying a historical event, composing a reply-hymn |

### No Check Required

- Ashara-translated dialogue (she handles the hard part)
- Ring impressions (automatic — emotional only)
- Receiving Vel'thara transmissions (passive — everyone near the aperture feels it)
