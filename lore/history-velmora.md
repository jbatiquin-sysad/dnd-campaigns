# Vel'mora — The Western Continent

> *As told by Saya, Elder of the Ahm-vel (River Folk) at Harbor 1, to the Dawnwatch over three evenings. Translated by Pip Tanglethorn and Orindel Ashwick. Recorded Day 146–148, Goldmere 1254 AS.*

---

## The Name

The River Folk call the continent **Vel'mora** — literally *"people's land"* or *"the land that holds the people."* The name is shared by all five races, though each pronounces it with their own tonal inflection. It is one of the few words that is identical across all five languages — suggesting it predates the divergence of the races.

Saya says: *"Vel'mora is not a name we gave the land. It is the name the land gave us. The elders say the ground hummed it before we had words."*

---

## Geography

Vel'mora is roughly **2,400 miles east-to-west and 1,200 miles north-to-south** (confirmed by the World's planetary map). The terrain divides into five broad regions, each associated with one of the five races:

| Region | Terrain | Primary Race | Key Features |
|--------|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **The River Valleys** (east) | Temperate forest, wide river deltas, flood plains | River Folk (Ahm-vel) | Dozens of rivers flowing east to the sea. Rich soil. Stilt villages along every major waterway. |
| **The Green Table** (center) | Vast open plains, tall grass, scattered groves | Tall Ones (Toh-vel) | Flat, fertile, enormous. Farming settlements. The breadbasket of Vel'mora. |
| **The Singing Peaks** (west-central) | Mountain range, high valleys, deep caves | Stone Singers (Ren-vel) | The spine of the continent. Peaks reach 15,000+ ft. The Stone Singers live inside the mountains. |
| **The Running Grass** (north) | Northern plains, steppe, seasonal frost | Wind Runners (Kah-vel) | Endless grassland. Herds of large grazing animals. The Wind Runners follow them. |
| **The Drowned Coast** (south/southwest) | Rocky coast, sea caves, tidal flats, subterranean networks | Deep Ones (Sol-vel) | Where the mountains meet the sea. Caves, tunnels, underwater passages. The Deep Ones live between stone and water. |

**The Deep Forest** (interior, between the Green Table and the Singing Peaks): Dense, ancient, canopy 200+ ft in places. Avoided by all five races. Home of the **kah-sol.**

### Rivers

The continent is defined by its rivers. Saya names seven major waterways:

| River | Direction | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| **Ahm-toh** (Sky River) | North to east coast | Largest. The River Folk's primary waterway. Harbor 1 sits at its mouth. |
| **Ahm-ren** (Earth River) | Central, east-flowing | Runs along the southern edge of the Green Table. Tall Ones farm its banks. |
| **Ahm-sol** (Dark River) | Southwest, underground sections | Flows through Stone Singer territory. Parts run underground for miles. |
| **Ahm-kah** (Forest River) | West, into the Deep Forest | No one follows it past the forest boundary. |
| 3 smaller tributaries | Various | Named but not yet mapped by the Dawnwatch. |

## Seasons & Climate

Vel'mora has **four seasons**, similar in rhythm to the eastern continent but with regional variation:

| Season | Months (approx.) | Weather | Notes |
|--------|------------------|---------|-------|
| **Toh-ahm** (Sky-Water / Rain Season) | Early spring | Heavy rains, river flooding, planting season | The River Folk plant in the flood. The rivers sing loudest. |
| **Toh-ren** (Sky-Earth / Warm Season) | Late spring–summer | Warm, dry, long days. Peak growing. | Travel season. Trade between races. The Green Table turns gold. |
| **Kah-toh** (Forest-Sky / Turning Season) | Autumn | Cooling, leaves turn, harvest. First frosts in the north. | **The kah-sol is most active.** All races pull back from the deep forest. Harvest festivals. |
| **Sol-ren** (Dark-Earth / Cold Season) | Winter | Cold, snow in the mountains and north. Rivers slow. | The Stone Singers seal their passes. The Deep Ones go deeper. The Wind Runners move south. |

**Current season:** Late Toh-ren (Warm Season), transitioning to Kah-toh (Turning Season). The Dawnwatch arrived in high summer. Autumn is approaching.

**Climate notes:**
- Eastern coast (River Folk territory): temperate, maritime influence, mild winters, wet springs.
- Central plains (Tall Ones): continental, hot summers, cold winters, dramatic storms.
- Mountains (Stone Singers): alpine, snow 8 months/year at high elevation, sheltered valleys.
- Northern plains (Wind Runners): steppe, harsh winters, short hot summers, constant wind.
- Southern coast (Deep Ones): mild, wet, fog-heavy, tidal extremes.

## The Five Races — Diplomatic Standing

Saya speaks carefully here. She is an elder — she knows the difference between what is true and what is polite.

### Ahm-vel — The River Folk
- **Population:** ~120,000
- **Government:** Elder councils. Each village has an elder (like Saya). Major decisions are made at seasonal gatherings where elders from dozens of villages meet and *sing* consensus — a tonal voting system where pitch indicates agreement.
- **Character:** Traders, fishers, builders. Open, curious, hospitable. The most outward-facing of the five races. They trade with everyone.
- **Relations with others:**
  - Tall Ones: **Strong alliance.** Trade partners. River Folk fish and craft; Tall Ones farm and herd. Intermarriage is common along the Ahm-ren border.
  - Stone Singers: **Respectful distance.** Trade at mountain-foot markets twice a year. The Stone Singers are private. The River Folk do not push.
  - Wind Runners: **Friendly but infrequent.** The Wind Runners pass through River Folk territory seasonally. They trade hides and bone for fish and reed-work. Relations are warm but shallow — the Wind Runners do not stay.
  - Deep Ones: **Wary respect.** The Deep Ones trade rarely and speak less. They appear at the southern river-mouths once a year with strange goods — deep-sea shells, bioluminescent coral, metals the River Folk cannot identify. They trade silently and leave. Saya says: *"They know things. They do not share. We do not ask."*

### Toh-vel — The Tall Ones
- **Population:** ~100,000
- **Government:** Farming communes led by **Rememberers** — elders who maintain oral histories spanning thousands of years. Decisions are slow, deliberate, rooted in precedent.
- **Character:** Patient, agrarian, deeply traditional. They are the largest race physically — averaging 6'6". They farm the Green Table with techniques passed down for millennia. They do not innovate; they *refine.*
- **Saya's assessment:** *"They are the ground. They do not move. They do not change. But they remember everything. If you want to know what Vel'mora was like a thousand years ago, ask a Rememberer."*

### Ren-vel — The Stone Singers
- **Population:** ~40,000
- **Government:** Unknown to the River Folk in detail. Saya says they are led by **Resonance Elders** — individuals who can hear the mountain's voice. Their governance is tied to the stone itself.
- **Character:** Private, resonance-sensitive, master craftspeople. They carve the mountains from within. Their cities are underground — vast, echoing, carved with geometric precision. They hear the World (faintly, unconsciously, like the dwarves of the eastern continent).
- **Saya's assessment:** *"They are the closest to the ground's voice. They sealed their passes against the kah-sol two hundred years ago. They know what it is. They will not tell us."*
- **Relations:** Trade at mountain-foot markets. Polite. Guarded. They sell carved stone, refined metals, and resonance crystals (raw, unstructured). They buy food, textiles, and river-reed.

### Kah-vel — The Wind Runners
- **Population:** ~30,000
- **Government:** **Herd-leaders** — the fastest, most skilled rider leads. Leadership changes with the seasons and the herds. Fluid, meritocratic, impermanent.
- **Character:** Nomadic, fast, joyful. They ride large herd animals (Saya describes something like elk-horses — tall, antlered, fast). They follow the grazing herds across the northern plains in an annual circuit. They value speed, freedom, and laughter.
- **Saya's assessment:** *"They are the wind. You cannot hold them. You cannot predict them. But when they come through, they bring news from every corner of Vel'mora. They are the continent's messengers, whether they intend to be or not."*
- **Relations:** Friendly with everyone. Enemies with no one. They pass through all territories and are welcomed everywhere — a nomadic neutrality that has held for centuries.

### Sol-vel — The Deep Ones
- **Population:** ~10,000
- **Government:** Unknown. Saya says: *"They do not explain themselves."*
- **Character:** Reclusive, ancient, unsettling. They live in the spaces between stone and water — sea caves, tidal tunnels, subterranean rivers. They are shorter than the other races, pale, with large dark eyes adapted to low light. They are the only race that enters the Deep Forest deliberately.
- **Saya's assessment:** *"They are the oldest. They were here before the rest of us — or so they say. They know things about Vel'mora that the ground itself has forgotten. They trade with us once a year. They bring things from the deep — metals, shells, coral that glows. They take food and silence. They do not want our company. They want to be left alone."*
- **The kah-sol connection:** The Deep Ones enter the Deep Forest and return. No other race can say this. They will not explain how or why. Saya believes they have some arrangement or understanding with the kah-sol — or they know something about it that gives them safe passage.

## History — As Told by the Elders

Saya prefaces this: *"We do not write. We sing. What I tell you is what was sung to me, and to my mother, and to her mother. The song is old. Some notes may have changed. But the melody is true."*

### The First Age — "When the Ground Sang"
*Time unknown — thousands of years ago.*

The elders say that in the beginning, the ground of Vel'mora sang. Not metaphorically — the earth produced sound, resonance, vibration. The first people (all five races claim to be "first") emerged from the land itself — born from stone, river, wind, forest, and deep water. They could hear the ground's song and they shaped their lives around it.

The five races diverged naturally — each drawn to the terrain that matched their nature. The River Folk followed the water. The Tall Ones rooted in the plains. The Stone Singers went into the mountains. The Wind Runners chased the horizon. The Deep Ones descended.

**Rememberer Orath's addition (Tall Ones):** "The races were not just drawn — they were *called.* The ground sang differently in each region, and each race followed the song that matched their nature. The Tall Ones followed the song of *growth* — slow, patient, cyclical. We did not choose the plains. The plains chose us."

*"We were one people once. The ground sang one song. We each heard a different part."*

### The Second Age — "The Quiet"
*Approximately 1,000–2,000 years ago (Saya is uncertain).*

The ground's song faded. Gradually, over generations, the resonance that had defined Vel'mora grew quieter. The geometric patterns that the races carved into their tools and buildings still worked — but less reliably. The Stone Singers retreated deeper into their mountains, chasing the fading sound. The Deep Ones went deeper underground.

Saya says: *"The elders say the ground did not stop singing. It grew tired. Or sad. Or it was listening for something that never came."*

**Rememberer Orath's addition:** "The Quiet hit the Tall Ones hardest. Our crop yields dropped by a third over two generations. We compensated by developing the concentric-ring rotation system — mathematical optimization to replace the resonance boost we'd lost. It worked. But we never solved it with the ground. The ground stopped helping and we learned to do without. But we never stopped listening."

**Dawnwatch note (Sylvara):** This timeline roughly corresponds to the period after the Gardener landed on the eastern continent and the ward network was established — approximately 3,200 years ago. The Gardener's arrival may have shifted the planet's resonance balance eastward, inadvertently weakening the western continent's raw geometric field. The ground didn't stop singing — the song was redirected.

### The Third Age — "The Taking"
*Approximately 800–1,000 years ago.*

The kah-sol appeared. Or was noticed. The elders are unclear on whether it arrived or was always there and simply became active when the ground's song weakened.

**Rememberer Orath's addition (Tall Ones):** "The kah-sol arrived during a meteor shower. The Rememberer of that time recorded it — a streak of fire across the autumn sky, and then silence from the deep forest for a full year. When the silence ended, the taking began." This confirms the Stone Singers' account of an 800-year-old impact scar in the bedrock and narrows the arrival to a specific astronomical event.

The Deep Forest — always dense, always avoided for practical reasons — became dangerous. People who entered did not return. At first it was rare. Then it was regular. Two or three per year, across all five races. Hunters, foragers, children who wandered too far.

The five races responded differently:
- The River Folk established boundary patrols and tonal warning posts.
- The Tall Ones moved their farms further from the forest edge.
- The Stone Singers sealed their mountain passes — the most dramatic response.
- The Wind Runners simply rode around it.
- The Deep Ones... entered it. And returned. And said nothing.

**Orath's addition:** "The Wind Runners are the continent's memory of movement. We remember by staying. They remember by going. Every year they complete the circuit — north to south, east to west — and they carry news, seeds, stories, and children between all five races. Without them, we would each be alone."

*"We have lived with the kah-sol for longer than anyone alive can remember. It is part of Vel'mora now — the part we do not speak of at festivals. But it is always there. The forest that does not give back."*

### The Present — "The Waiting"
*Current era.*

Saya says the River Folk have a prophecy — or not a prophecy, exactly. A song. An old song, older than the kah-sol, from the First Age. It says:

*"When the ground sings again, the people from the sunrise will come. They will carry the song in their hands. And the forest will give back what it took."*

She did not mention this song until the third evening. She looked at Zaryth when she said it. At the Diadem. At the Heart-Fruit. At the faint ward-glow on his gauntlets.

*"I did not believe the song. My mother did not believe it. Her mother did not believe it. But the ground sang when you arrived. The fish jumped. The water stilled. And you carry the song in your hands."*

She paused.

*"I am old enough to know that songs are not always true. But I am also old enough to know that sometimes they are."*

- **Sylvara:** The "Quiet" corresponds to the Gardener's arrival on the eastern continent. The ward network may have drawn resonance eastward. Planting seeds on Vel'mora could restore the balance — reawaken the ground's song. The 23 planting sites are the key.

- **Mira:** The Deep Ones are the intelligence priority. They enter the kah-sol's territory and survive. They know what it is. Getting them to talk is the challenge.
- **Orindel:** The prophecy song uses First Age tonal intervals — older than anything else in the River Folk repertoire. It's not a later addition. It's genuinely ancient.
