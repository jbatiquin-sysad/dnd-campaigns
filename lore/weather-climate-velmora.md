# Weather & Climate of Vel'mora

> *As told by Rememberer Orath of the Ahm-ren eastern commune, supplemented by Saya (River Folk), Ren-tha (Stone Singers), and Kash (River Folk guide). Compiled by Torven Ashwick and Corwin Hale. Day 210, 1254 AS.*

---

## Seasonal Overview

Vel'mora has four seasons, locally named. The climate varies significantly by region but follows a consistent continental pattern.

| Season | Local Name | Approx. Days | Character |
|--------|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Rain Season** | Toh-ahm (Sky-Water) | Days 1–90 | Heavy rains, river flooding, planting. Warmth building. |
| **Warm Season** | Toh-ren (Sky-Earth) | Days 91–180 | Hot, dry, long days. Peak growing. Travel season. |
| **Turning Season** | Kah-toh (Forest-Sky) | Days 181–270 | Cooling, harvest, first frosts north. Kah-sol most active. |
| **Cold Season** | Sol-ren (Dark-Earth) | Days 271–360 | Cold, snow in mountains/north. Rivers slow. Settlements hunker. |

**Year length:** 360 days (same as eastern continent — confirmed by Corwin's astronomical observations).

## Regional Climate

### River Valleys (East Coast) — Ahm-vel Territory
- **Climate:** Maritime temperate. Moderated by ocean.
- **Rain Season:** Heavy — rivers flood, stilt villages designed for it. 40–60 days of rain.
- **Warm Season:** Warm, humid, 75–85°F. Best fishing. Sea breezes.
- **Turning Season:** Mild cooling. 55–70°F. Harvest. Light rain returns.
- **Cold Season:** Cool, not cold. 40–55°F. Rivers slow but don't freeze. Light frost possible inland. Snow extremely rare at coast.
- **Hazards:** Flooding (Rain Season), occasional coastal storms (Turning/Cold), fog (Cold Season).

### Green Table (Central Plains) — Toh-vel Territory
- **Climate:** Continental. Extreme seasonal variation.
- **Rain Season:** Moderate rain, snowmelt from mountains feeds rivers. Planting begins mid-season. 50–65°F.
- **Warm Season:** Hot. 85–100°F. Dry. Thunderstorms (dramatic, brief, violent). Grain grows fast.
- **Turning Season:** Rapid cooling. 40–65°F. Harvest. First frost by Day 220. Tallgrass turns golden.
- **Cold Season:** Cold. 10–35°F. Snow cover 60–90 days. Rivers freeze at edges. Wind from the north is brutal.
- **Hazards:** Thunderstorms (Warm), early frost (Turning — can destroy unharvested grain), blizzards (Cold), prairie fires (Warm/Turning — tallgrass is flammable).
- **Orath's note:** "I have seen 80 winters. The coldest froze the Ahm-ren solid for 30 days. The hottest killed a third of the grain. The land forgives neither carelessness nor arrogance."

### Singing Peaks (Mountains) — Ren-vel Territory
- **Climate:** Alpine. Harsh.
- **Rain Season:** Snow at elevation, rain in valleys. Passes blocked by snow until late season.
- **Warm Season:** Brief. 50–70°F in valleys, below freezing at peaks. Only season passes are naturally open (before the Stone Singers sealed them).
- **Turning Season:** Snow returns early. Passes close by Day 220.
- **Cold Season:** Severe. -10 to 20°F. Heavy snow. The Stone Singers live underground — the surface is uninhabitable 6 months/year.
- **Hazards:** Avalanche (Rain/Turning), rockfall, altitude sickness, hypothermia, whiteout blizzards.
- **Ren-tha's note (via Keth):** "The mountain does not have weather. The mountain *is* weather. We live inside it because the outside is not for the living in winter."

### Running Grass (Northern Plains) — Kah-vel Territory
- **Climate:** Steppe. Windy, dry, extreme.
- **Rain Season:** Minimal rain. Snowmelt. Grass greens. Herds calve.
- **Warm Season:** Hot days (85–95°F), cool nights (50–60°F). Constant wind. Herds graze north.
- **Turning Season:** Rapid cooling. Herds move south. Wind increases. First snow by Day 240.
- **Cold Season:** Brutal. -20 to 15°F. Wind chill extreme. Snow and ice. The Wind Runners move to southern steppe edge — still cold, but survivable with herd-warmth and hide shelters.
- **Hazards:** Wind (constant, 20–40 mph average, gusts to 70), dust storms (Warm), blizzards (Cold), lightning (Warm — the plains are flat and exposed).
- **Orath's note:** "The Wind Runners do not fight the weather. They move with it. That is why they survive where no one else could."

### Drowned Coast (South/Southwest) — Sol-vel Territory
- **Climate:** Maritime, mild, wet.
- **Rain Season:** Very wet. Tidal extremes. Sea caves flood.
- **Warm Season:** Mild. 65–80°F. Fog-heavy. Calm seas.
- **Turning Season:** Cooling, increasing storms. Tidal surges.
- **Cold Season:** Cool, wet. 35–50°F. Storms. The Deep Ones go deeper underground — the surface coast is battered.
- **Hazards:** Tidal flooding, sea storms, fog (year-round), rip currents, cave collapse during storms.
- **Saya's note:** "The Deep Ones live where the sea argues with the stone. I do not know how they survive. I think they do not care about surviving. They care about knowing."

### Deep Forest (Interior) — Kah-sol Territory
- **Climate:** Temperate forest, moderated by canopy.
- **Rain Season:** Wet. The canopy traps moisture — perpetual drip.
- **Warm Season:** Warm, humid, dark beneath the canopy. 70–85°F. The canopy blocks most direct sunlight.
- **Turning Season:** The canopy holds heat longer than open ground — the forest stays warm when the plains cool. **This is why the kah-sol is most active in autumn — the forest is warmer than its surroundings, and the temperature differential drives movement outward.**
- **Cold Season:** The canopy insulates. The forest floor rarely freezes. 35–50°F when the plains are below zero.
- **Hazards:** The kah-sol. Also: disorientation (no sky visible), flooding (Rain Season, forest floor), falling branches (200+ ft canopy), unknown fauna.
- **Orath's critical observation:** "The forest is always warmer than the land around it. In autumn, when the cold comes, the warmth pushes outward. The kah-sol rides the warmth. That is why the taking increases in the Turning Season — the forest breathes out, and the kah-sol breathes with it."

## Weather Patterns — Key Phenomena

### The Autumn Breath
Orath's term for the temperature differential between the Deep Forest and the surrounding plains/mountains in the Turning Season. As the continent cools, the forest retains heat, creating an outward airflow — warm, humid air pushing from the forest interior toward the cooler edges. This "breath" carries spores, pollen, moisture — and, according to all five races, the kah-sol's influence. The taking increases during the Autumn Breath because the kah-sol's network extends along the warm-air corridors.

**Torven's note:** "This is a thermal inversion pattern. The forest acts as a heat reservoir. The kah-sol has adapted to use the outward airflow as an extension mechanism. If we could cool the forest edge — a ward-based temperature barrier — we might be able to contain it."

### The Spring Flood
The Ahm-toh and Ahm-ren flood every Rain Season — predictably, reliably. The River Folk and Tall Ones have built their entire civilizations around this cycle. The flood deposits nutrient-rich silt on the plains (the Green Table's fertility) and fills the river deltas (the River Folk's fish runs). Disrupting the flood cycle would be catastrophic.

### The Mountain Storms
The Singing Peaks generate their own weather — orographic lift creates intense localized storms on the eastern slopes. These storms feed the Ahm-ren and its tributaries. Orath: "The mountains make the rain that feeds the grain that feeds the people. Everything begins with the stone."

### The Wind Runner Circuit
The Wind Runners' annual migration follows the weather — north in summer (following the grass growth), south in autumn/winter (ahead of the cold). Their circuit is a living weather map — where the herds go, the season follows. Orath: "When the Wind Runners pass heading south, you have twenty days before the first hard frost. They have never been wrong."

## Seasonal Calendar — Cross-Reference with Eastern Continent

| Vel'mora Season | Days | Eastern Equivalent | Notes |
|----------------|------|--------------------|-------|
| Toh-ahm (Rain) | 1–90 | Greenthaw–Highsun | Both continents: spring, planting, thaw |
| Toh-ren (Warm) | 91–180 | Solstice–Reaptide | Both: summer, peak activity, travel |
| Kah-toh (Turning) | 181–270 | Ashfall–Frostmere | Both: autumn, harvest, cooling. Kah-sol active. |
| Sol-ren (Cold) | 271–360 | Deepwinter–Thawsend | Both: winter, dormancy, survival |

The calendars align almost perfectly — same 360-day year, same seasonal rhythm. Corwin confirms the astronomical basis: same planet, same orbital period, same axial tilt. The seasons are synchronized across both continents.
