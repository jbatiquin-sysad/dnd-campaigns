## 1. Overview — The Transformation (1247–1256 AS)

Nine years ago, the Hearthcoast economy was a rigged game.

The Unchained Compact — a seven-city conspiracy of corrupt officials, guild masters, and shadow brokers — controlled trade routes, suppressed wages, manipulated currency, and siphoned wealth from every settlement between Greymoor and Veldmere. The ward network had been failing for over a thousand years, Dead Zones pressed inward, and safe arable land shrank with every generation. Knowledge was hoarded in Aelindra. Resources were extracted from Greymoor. Coastal villages starved while port cities grew fat on monopoly margins.

The Dawnwatch changed that. Not through conquest — through *work*.

### What Changed

| Factor | Before (1247) | Now (1256) | Impact |
|--------|---------------|------------|--------|
| Ward network | 3 of 14 nodes partially active | **ALL 14 nodes + 104 plantings active** | Dead Zones receding. New safe land. Agricultural yields doubled near Node 7. |
| Trade routes | Controlled by Compact agents | **10 independent routes + 3 autonomous vessels** | Prices stabilized. Competition restored. New goods from 3 continents. |
| Corrupt infrastructure | 7 cities compromised | **All 7 dismantled** | Civic trust restored. Fair taxation. Guild reform. |
| Intercontinental contact | None (1,248 years of isolation) | **Vel'mora + Kapuluan + Vel'thara** | Entirely new markets. Sea-silk, singing granite, fire opals, piña fiber. |
| Deep Road | Non-existent | **9-hour Fenhollow ↔ Aelindra transit** | Was 3.5 days overland. Knowledge and goods flow freely. |
| Agricultural ward | None | **Template #3 on Node 7 — double crops** | Fenhollow region transformed from subsistence to surplus. |
| Currency manipulation | Active (Coinwright's Guild, Harrowgate) | **Eliminated** | Stable exchange rates across all cities. |
| Knowledge access | Restricted (Aelindra Archive sealed) | **Open (Veyra Solund's reforms)** | Salthollow university revitalized. Literacy programs expanding. |

### The Numbers

- **Annual Dawnwatch fleet revenue:** ~19,740 gp
- **Monthly homestead surplus:** ~2,165 gp (income ~2,479 gp, expenses ~314 gp)
- **Party reserves:** ~52,490 gp
- **Monthly fleet revenue breakdown:** ~1,645 gp across 10 routes + 3 autonomous vessels
- **Regional GDP estimate (all Hearthcoast cities):** ~480,000 gp/year (up from ~310,000 gp in 1247)

That last figure is Thessaly's estimate based on port ledgers, market surveys, and tax records from allied civic authorities. It's rough — but the direction is unmistakable. The Hearthcoast is ~55% wealthier than it was nine years ago, and the growth is accelerating.

## 2. City-by-City Assessment

### Veldmere — Population ~18,000 (↑ Growing)

**Primary Industries:** Southern sea trade, shipbuilding, textiles, warehousing, fish processing
**Trend:** Strong growth. Population up ~2,000 since 1247.

Veldmere was Marchion Sulwick's domain — a trade empire built on monopoly tariffs and intimidation. When the Compact fell, the city stumbled for about eighteen months as the old power structures collapsed. Then it *boomed*.

With fair tariffs restored and Harbormaster Lenne Cray running legitimate customs, Veldmere has become the Hearthcoast's premier southern port. The Salt Maren runs Veldmere–Duskport (~110 gp/month), the Triangle Route touches Veldmere on every circuit (~150 gp/month), and the Blacktide Runner connects Ashport's tropical goods (~100 gp/month). Three Dawnwatch routes converge here — more than any other city.

**Quality of life:** Good and improving. Dockworkers' wages are up ~30% since the Compact fell. The fish processing district has expanded. New construction visible along the southern waterfront. The old Sulwick compound has been converted to a public market hall.

**Gaps:** Housing pressure from population growth. Rents in the dock quarter have risen faster than wages for unskilled laborers. The city needs a proper healer's hall — the temple district handles overflow, but it's stretched. Sewage infrastructure in the lower wards predates the Reconstruction and needs replacement.

### Harrowgate — Population ~15,000 (↑ Growing)

**Primary Industries:** Overland trade, river commerce, banking, crafts, agriculture
**Trend:** Steady growth. The largest market in the region.

Harrowgate sits at the crossroads of the King's Road and the Mirren River — every overland route passes through it. The Coinwright's Guild manipulation under Guildmaster Tetch suppressed honest commerce for years. Since the Guild was frozen and reformed, Harrowgate's markets have returned to genuine price discovery.

The city benefits from *every* trade route indirectly. Goods from Veldmere, Aelindra, Greymoor, and the coast all pass through Harrowgate's markets. The Dawnwatch Coach (overland resonance-assisted transport) has improved transit times. River barges carry Greymoor iron and Fenhollow agricultural surplus.

**Quality of life:** Good. Harrowgate has always been the most economically diverse city on the Hearthcoast. The reformed banking sector means small merchants can actually get fair credit. Alderman Kellan's civic reforms have improved tax transparency.

**Gaps:** The city's prosperity is unevenly distributed. The river quarter — where barge workers, porters, and day laborers live — hasn't seen the same improvement as the merchant district. Harrowgate has no university and limited access to higher education. Children of laborers have few paths to skilled work. Winter is hard on the river quarter — flooding is annual, and the city has never properly addressed it.

### Duskport — Population ~12,000 (→ Stable)

**Primary Industries:** Harbor trade, temple services, fishing, shipbuilding, civic administration
**Trend:** Stable. Recovering from Compact damage but not yet growing.

Duskport is where the Dawnwatch began — where House Aldenmere ran its shadow operation through the Vel Noctis. The city's recovery has been slower than Veldmere's. The temple district anchors civic life, and Magistrate Sera Voss has been a steady hand, but Duskport lacks a signature economic driver.

The city handles coastal trade between Veldmere and the northern ports. It's a waypoint — ships stop, resupply, and move on. The Salt Maren's Veldmere–Duskport run (~110 gp/month) keeps goods flowing, but Duskport doesn't originate much trade of its own.

**Quality of life:** Adequate. The temple district provides basic healing and charity. Vael Orun's network supports the poorest families. But there's a sense of stagnation — young people leave for Veldmere or Harrowgate.

**Gaps:** Duskport needs an economic identity beyond "waypoint." The shipbuilding yards are underutilized. The Undercroft (former Vel Noctis base) is sealed but could be repurposed. The city has no direct connection to the new intercontinental goods — everything arrives secondhand through Veldmere at markup. Healthcare is temple-dependent with no secular healers' guild. Education is informal.

### Sunmere — Population ~10,000+ (↑↑ Fast Growing)

**Primary Industries:** Drow trade nexus, resonance fabric, spider-silk, alchemy, cultural exchange
**Trend:** Rapid growth. The surprise story of the decade.

Nobody predicted Sunmere. A mid-sized southern city that was economically unremarkable before 1248 has become the Hearthcoast's most dynamic market — because of the Drow.

House Vel'quinar's integration into surface commerce, combined with the Vel'sharen Knowledge Accord and the spider-silk resonance fabric industry (developed by Thessaly Ironveil and the Vel'quinar weavers), created an entirely new luxury goods sector. Resonance fabric commands premium prices in every city. Sunmere is the production and distribution hub.

The Drow trade nexus also channels underground goods — resonance moss, deep minerals, and alchemical reagents — that were previously unavailable on the surface. The Vel'sharen joint laboratory produces research materials that Aelindra's scholars pay handsomely for.

**Quality of life:** Improving rapidly, but uneven. The Drow quarter is prosperous and well-organized. Surface residents near the trade district benefit from new employment. But the city's rapid growth has outpaced infrastructure — water supply, waste management, and housing are all strained. Cultural tensions between long-time residents and the new Drow community exist, though they're managed better here than anywhere else on the Hearthcoast.

**Gaps:** Sunmere desperately needs infrastructure investment. The water system serves 6,000 — not 10,000+. Housing construction can't keep pace with immigration. The city has no formal education system; children learn through apprenticeship or not at all. Healthcare is split between surface and Drow traditions with no integration. The rapid wealth concentration in the trade district hasn't reached the fishing quarter on the eastern shore.

### Aelindra — Population ~8,000 (↑ Growing)

**Primary Industries:** Knowledge trade, Archive services, scholarship, rare goods, enchantment
**Trend:** Growing. Transformed by the Archive reforms and the Deep Road.

For a thousand years, Aelindra hoarded knowledge and charged for access. Archivist-General Thessara Kain's Sealed Quorum controlled what scholars could study, what texts could be copied, and who could enter the Archive. The city's economy was built on artificial scarcity.

Veyra Solund's reforms changed that. The Archive is now genuinely open. Scholars come from across the Hearthcoast — and increasingly from Vel'mora and Kapuluan — to study. The Northern Express route (Eilistraee's Grace, ~150 gp/month) brings knowledge materials and rare goods. The Deep Road connects Aelindra to Fenhollow in 9 hours — a journey that took 3.5 days overland.

The knowledge economy is real and growing. Copying services, translation work, research commissions, enchantment services, and scholarly tourism all generate revenue. Aelindra's cliff-side location limits physical expansion, but the city is denser and more prosperous than it's been in centuries.

**Quality of life:** Good for scholars and skilled workers. The Archive employs hundreds directly. But Aelindra has always been a two-tier city — the cliff-top scholars and the harbor-level laborers who keep the city fed and supplied. The harbor district is cramped, poorly maintained, and largely ignored by the civic council.

**Gaps:** The harbor district needs investment — housing, sanitation, a proper market. Aelindra imports almost all its food; a supply disruption would be catastrophic within days. The city has no agricultural land and depends entirely on trade. Winter isolation remains a problem — when sea storms close the northern route, Aelindra relies on stored provisions and the Deep Road. The Deep Road is a single point of failure. Education is excellent for those who can afford it and nonexistent for those who can't — the Archive's "open access" still requires literacy.

### Greymoor — Population ~7,000 (→ Stable, At Risk)

**Primary Industries:** Mining (iron, silver), smelting, stonework
**Trend:** Stable but fragile. The most vulnerable major city.

Greymoor was the Compact's extraction engine. Dorn Blackveil ran the mines, suppressed wages, falsified geological surveys, and shipped raw ore east at prices that enriched everyone except the miners. When the Compact fell, the mines were in poor condition — safety violations, exhausted upper shafts, and a workforce that had been ground down for years.

Torben Vask's reinstatement as crew chief and Judge Kess's cooperation have stabilized operations. Iron and silver still flow. But Greymoor faces a structural problem: the upper shafts are depleting. The city needs to invest in deeper mining infrastructure or find new economic activity. Neither is happening fast enough.

The Iron Road connects Greymoor to Harrowgate, and Greymoor iron feeds the entire Hearthcoast's tool and construction needs. But the city captures very little of the final value — raw ore leaves Greymoor at 3–5 gp per ton, and finished tools sell in Harrowgate at 10x that margin.

**Quality of life:** Poor relative to other cities. Mining is dangerous work. The miners' quarter has the highest injury rate and lowest life expectancy on the Hearthcoast. Healthcare is limited to Sister Maren's chapel and a single overworked herbalist. Wages have improved since the Compact fell, but Greymoor remains the poorest major city per capita.

**Gaps:** This is where common folk are hurting most. Greymoor needs: (1) a proper healers' hall — mining injuries are constant and often crippling; (2) a smithing guild or finishing works so the city can sell tools, not just ore; (3) deeper shaft investment or alternative industries; (4) a school — there is none; (5) better road maintenance on the Iron Road, which becomes impassable in Greymist and stays that way until Greenthaw. Winter in Greymoor is genuinely dangerous for the poor.

### Ashport — Population ~6,000 (↑ Growing)

**Primary Industries:** Fishing, timber, tropical agriculture, citrus, shipbuilding
**Trend:** Moderate growth. Benefiting from southern trade expansion.

Ashport was always the Hearthcoast's quiet southern edge — warm, remote, and largely self-sufficient. The Blacktide Runner's Ashport–Veldmere route (~100 gp/month) connected it to the broader economy for the first time in a meaningful way. Timber and citrus flow north; manufactured goods and iron tools flow south.

The Kapuluan trade accord has been transformative for Ashport. As the nearest Hearthcoast port to the southern archipelago, Ashport is becoming a secondary hub for tropical goods. Fire opals, deep coral, and piña fiber pass through Ashport when the Alon ng Araw makes its Kapuluan run. Local merchants are learning to handle exotic goods and developing relationships with Kapuluan traders.

**Quality of life:** Decent and improving. Ashport's tropical climate means winter hardship is less severe than northern cities. Food is abundant — fish, fruit, and timber are all local. The main limitation is isolation; Ashport is far from Harrowgate's markets and Aelindra's knowledge.

**Gaps:** Ashport has no healer beyond a village herbalist. Serious injuries or illness mean a multi-day journey to Duskport or Veldmere. Education is nonexistent — children learn fishing and timber work from parents. The city has no proper harbor infrastructure; ships anchor in the bay and lighter goods ashore. A real dock would double Ashport's trade capacity. The timber industry is unregulated — Torven has noted unsustainable cutting rates in the eastern groves.

### Salthollow — Population ~5,000 (↑ Growing)

**Primary Industries:** Education (university, Athenaeum), scholarship, salt production, brewing
**Trend:** Growing. The university revival is driving immigration.

Lorekeeper Vane's corruption hollowed out Salthollow's university for years — stipends diverted, faculty intimidated, research suppressed. Councilwoman Brynn Ashworth's reforms and the Compact's fall have restored the institution. Professor Elara Dunwick returned from retirement. New faculty are arriving. Student enrollment is up.

The Northern Express (Eilistraee's Grace) connects Salthollow to Aelindra's Archive, creating a knowledge corridor that didn't exist before. Scholars shuttle between the two cities. The Athenaeum is expanding its collection. Salthollow's salt production — from the coastal flats — provides a steady export base.

**Quality of life:** Good for scholars and students. The university creates a literate, engaged civic culture. But Salthollow is small, and the salt workers on the flats live a different life than the scholars on the hill. Salt production is hard, seasonal work with poor long-term health outcomes (joint damage, respiratory issues from salt dust).

**Gaps:** The salt workers need better working conditions and healthcare. The university's revival benefits the educated class but hasn't created pathways for working-class children. Salthollow's small size means limited market access — goods are expensive because everything is imported. Winter storms isolate the city for weeks at a time. The Athenaeum needs funding for preservation — some texts are deteriorating.

### Fenhollow — Population ~3,000 (↑↑ Fast Growing)

**Primary Industries:** Agriculture (ward-enhanced), alchemy, crafts, Dawnwatch homestead industries, education
**Trend:** Rapid growth. The most transformed settlement on the Hearthcoast.

Fenhollow was a marshland backwater of 1,500 people when the Dawnwatch arrived. Captain Jorik Brenn's militia was compromised. Old Harren Fenn's family had watched the wardhouse crumble for generations. The marsh was dangerous, the roads were bad, and young people left as soon as they could.

Now Fenhollow is the economic heart of a regional transformation. The agricultural ward on Node 7 (Template #3) doubles crop yields in a 10-mile radius. The Dawnwatch homestead — dairy, cider press, cheese cave, silver sage hybrid, apothecary, alchemy production line, glassware workshop, forge contracts — generates ~2,165 gp/month in surplus. The school educates 16 students (plus 5 graduated apprentices). The Dawnwatch Ensemble performs ward-resonant music. The Deep Road terminal connects to Aelindra in 9 hours.

Tidemeet, the river-mouth port, handles all seaborne trade. The Triangle Route, Northern Express, intercontinental vessels, and Kapuluan route all originate from Tidemeet. It's become the Hearthcoast's busiest port by vessel count, if not by tonnage.

**Quality of life:** Dramatically improved. Fenhollow residents have access to double-yield agriculture, an apothecary, a school, regular trade goods, and the protection of an active ward network. The Dawnwatch's 80/20 cooperative model with the Southern Shelf villages demonstrates that prosperity doesn't require exploitation. Wages for homestead workers are the highest per capita on the Hearthcoast.

**Gaps:** Fenhollow's transformation is real but fragile — it depends heavily on the Dawnwatch's continued presence and the ward network's stability. The marsh infrastructure (roads, bridges, drainage) still needs work. The population is growing faster than housing can be built. Tidemeet needs a proper harbor master and customs office as trade volume increases. The school serves 16 students — demand exceeds capacity threefold.

### Coral Haven — Population ~3,000 (→ Stable)

**Primary Industries:** Pearl diving, fishing, reef harvesting, shell crafts
**Trend:** Stable. Isolated but self-sufficient.

Coral Haven sits on a reef island, largely disconnected from Hearthcoast politics and trade. The Southern Shelf Cooperative's 3 boats occasionally reach Coral Haven, but it's not on any regular route. The island produces beautiful pearls and shell-work that command high prices — when they can reach a market.

**Quality of life:** Subsistence-level but culturally rich. Coral Haven's people are skilled divers and crafters. They eat well from the reef. But they have no access to iron tools, medicine, education, or news from the mainland. A bad storm season can be devastating with no external support.

**Gaps:** Coral Haven is the most underserved settlement we track. No healer. No school. No regular trade connection. No iron — tools are bone, shell, and coral. A single dedicated supply run per season would transform quality of life. This is a priority gap.

### Stormcape — 5 Cliff-Holds (↑ Growing)

**Holds:** Arn, Kell, Venn, Driftwood, Saltspray | **Combined Population:** ~1,200
**Primary Industries:** Salt harvesting, fishing, coastal foraging, storm glass (new)
**Trend:** Growing for the first time in generations.

The Stormcape cliff-holds were dying. Isolated on the western headland, cut off by Dead Zones, they survived on salt, fish, and stubbornness. Node 5's reconstruction (74%) and the Triangle Route changed everything. Iron tools — unavailable for years — now arrive regularly. Lamp oil, cloth, grain, and medicine flow west. Salt and fish flow east.

The pigeon network connects Stormcape to Tidemeet. The alliance with the Dawnwatch is genuine and deeply felt — Seren Ashvale, who kept Node 5 alive for 31 years alone, is one of them.

**Quality of life:** Improving from desperate to adequate. The cliff-holds now have iron tools, lamp oil, and grain stores for winter. But life on the western headland is still hard. The holds are small, exposed to storms, and far from any healer or school.

**Gaps:** No healer — injuries and illness are treated with folk remedies. No school. Winter storms still isolate the holds for weeks. The Triangle Route suspends its western leg in winter (Frostmere–Thawsend), leaving Stormcape on stored provisions for 3–4 months. A single bad winter could still be catastrophic. Storm glass production (from the volcanic western islands) is a potential new industry but needs investment in tools and training.

### Southern Shelf — 7 Villages (↑ Growing)

**Villages:** Saltbrine, Driftward, Kelpmere, Tidemouth, Shellhaven, Wavebreak, Farlight | **Combined Population:** ~2,800
**Primary Industries:** Fishing, kelp harvesting, pearl diving, shell crafts, small-scale agriculture
**Trend:** Growing. The cooperative model is working.

The Southern Shelf Cooperative is Thessaly's proudest achievement. Seven fishing villages, 3 boats, 12 crew, operating on an 80/20 split that favors the villages. The Dawnwatch takes ~20 gp/month — barely covering coordination costs. The villages keep the rest.

Pearls, kelp, and shell-carvings flow north to Tidemeet and Veldmere. Iron tools, grain, cloth, and medicine flow south. Grandmother's Promise (Planting #4, Tidemouth) synchronized all 7 village hearthstones, creating a ward-protected coastal corridor.

Edda Solvane serves as liaison. The cooperative has its own governance — the Dawnwatch advises but doesn't control.

**Quality of life:** The best it's ever been for these villages. Regular trade means iron tools, preserved food for winter, and basic medicine. The ward corridor means safer fishing and fewer monster incursions. But these are still small, remote fishing villages.

**Gaps:** No healer in any village — the nearest is Fenhollow or Veldmere, days away by boat. No school. Literacy is rare. Winter storms still damage boats and buildings with no resources for repair. Fresh water is a constant concern on the southern shelf — most villages rely on rain catchment. A single well-digging project would be transformative.

### Kapuluan — Southern Archipelago (~50,000) — Trade Partner

**Primary Industries:** Sailing, fire opals, deep coral, piña fiber, volcanic obsidian, cultural arts
**Relationship:** Allied. Trade accord sealed. Alon ng Araw (autonomous vessel) runs dedicated route.

The Kapuluan are a sailing culture of ~50,000 across a southern archipelago. First contact was made through the Bakunawa harmonization (Awit ng Tulay, nat 20) and the activation of Puso ng Dagat — a major resonance node in their waters. The trade accord brings fire opals, deep coral, piña fiber, and volcanic obsidian north (~350 gp/month via Alon ng Araw). Dark steel tools, storm glass, and alchemy go south.

The Kapuluan delegation at the Reaping Fair (1256) was a cultural sensation. Six Guro masters demonstrated arts unknown on the Hearthcoast. This is not just trade — it's the first new cultural exchange in over a thousand years.

**Assessment:** The Kapuluan relationship is healthy, reciprocal, and growing. Their sailing expertise exceeds ours. Their fire opals are the most valuable single trade good in the network. Priority: maintain trust, avoid extractive patterns, support their autonomy.

### Vel'mora — Western Continent (~300,000) — Trade Partner

**Primary Industries:** Sea-silk, singing granite, ren-kah ore, resonance crystals, advanced metallurgy
**Relationship:** Phase 1 contact complete. Two autonomous vessels (Jawbone + Velmoran) run intercontinental routes.

Vel'mora is a continent of ~300,000 people across 5 races, connected by the Jawbone (~320 gp/month, 26–28 day crossing) and the Velmoran (~445 gp/month, 20-day crossing). Eastbound goods — sea-silk, singing granite, ren-kah ore, resonance crystals — are unlike anything on the Hearthcoast. Westbound goods — storm glass, spider-silk, dark steel, alchemy — are equally novel to them.

The intercontinental routes are the highest-value trade in the network. Combined monthly revenue: ~765 gp — nearly half of total fleet income. The 20–28 day crossing means seasonal disruption: the western sea route suspends in winter (Frostmere–Longnight) when storms make the crossing too dangerous.

**Assessment:** Vel'mora trade is transformative but concentrated. Only Tidemeet handles intercontinental goods directly. Every other city pays a middleman markup. Distributing intercontinental goods more broadly — perhaps through a Veldmere or Duskport secondary depot — would spread the benefit.

### What the Fleet Changed

**Before the fleet (1247):** Trade between cities was controlled by Compact-affiliated merchants who set prices, restricted competition, and extracted monopoly rents. Stormcape couldn't buy iron. Greymoor couldn't sell ore at fair prices. Aelindra charged access fees for knowledge. Coastal villages had no market access at all.

**Now (1256):** Ten independent routes ensure that no single merchant or city controls trade flow. Prices are set by supply and demand, not by cartel. The Triangle Route alone broke Stormcape's isolation. The Northern Express broke Aelindra's knowledge monopoly. The intercontinental routes introduced goods that no Hearthcoast merchant had ever seen.

### Price Effects

| Good | Price (1247) | Price (1256) | Change | Reason |
|------|-------------|-------------|--------|--------|
| Iron tools (Stormcape) | 8–12 gp | 3–5 gp | ↓ 55% | Triangle Route competition |
| Grain (Aelindra) | 4–6 gp/bushel | 2–3 gp/bushel | ↓ 50% | Deep Road + Northern Express |
| Salt (inland cities) | 3–5 gp/barrel | 1–2 gp/barrel | ↓ 60% | Multiple coastal routes |
| Healing herbs (Fenhollow) | 2–4 gp/bundle | 1–2 gp/bundle | ↓ 50% | Agricultural ward surplus |
| Lamp oil (winter) | 5–8 gp/barrel | 3–4 gp/barrel | ↓ 45% | Veldmere supply + stockpiling |
| Sea-silk (new) | N/A | 15–25 gp/yard | New market | Intercontinental trade |
| Fire opals (new) | N/A | 50–200 gp each | New market | Kapuluan trade |
| Resonance fabric (new) | N/A | 20–40 gp/yard | New market | Vel'quinar spider-silk industry |

The pattern is clear: **staple goods are cheaper, and entirely new luxury goods have appeared.** The common folk benefit from the first trend. The second trend generates the revenue that funds the fleet, the homestead, and the ward network maintenance.

### Seasonal Vulnerability

The fleet's Achilles' heel is winter. From Frostmere through Longnight (Days 241–330), the western sea route suspends entirely. The Triangle Route drops its western leg. Only the Veldmere–Duskport run, the Deep Road, and overland routes operate reliably.

| Season | Active Routes | Monthly Revenue | Risk |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|------|
| Summer (Solstice–Reaptide) | All 10 | ~1,645 gp | Low |
| Autumn (Ashfall–Greymist) | 8–9 | ~1,300 gp | Moderate (storms) |
| Winter (Frostmere–Longnight) | 4–5 | ~600 gp | High (isolation) |
| Spring (Greenthaw–Highsun) | 8–10 | ~1,400 gp | Low–Moderate |

**Annual revenue adjusted for seasonality:** ~14,700–19,740 gp (depending on winter severity).

Winter is when common folk suffer most. Prices rise, supply drops, and isolated settlements — Stormcape, Coral Haven, the Southern Shelf — rely entirely on stored provisions. A bad winter with early storms can mean hunger.

## 4. New Markets — Intercontinental & Kapuluan Trade

### The Scale of What's New

For 1,248 years — since the Sundering — the Hearthcoast knew nothing beyond its own coastline. No contact with other continents. No knowledge of other civilizations. The ward network's collapse made ocean crossing suicidal; the Dead Zones and boundary thinning spawned creatures in the deep water that destroyed any vessel that ventured too far.

In nine years, the Dawnwatch has established contact with:

| Partner | Population | Distance | Crossing Time | Vessels | Monthly Revenue |

### The Distribution Problem

Here's the uncomfortable truth: **intercontinental goods only touch Tidemeet directly.** Every other city buys them secondhand, at markup, through Veldmere or Harrowgate merchants. A fire opal that arrives in Tidemeet at 50 gp sells in Harrowgate at 80 gp and in Greymoor at 120 gp — if it reaches Greymoor at all.

The common folk in most cities will never hold a fire opal or wear sea-silk. These are luxury goods. But the *revenue* from luxury trade funds the fleet, the homestead, the school, the ward maintenance, and the alchemy line that produces healing poultices at cost. The trick is making sure that revenue flows downward.

## 5. The Ward Effect

### How Restored Wards Create Prosperity

The ward network's restoration is the single largest economic driver on the Hearthcoast — larger than the fleet, larger than intercontinental trade, larger than the Compact's dismantling. Here's why:

**Safe land.** Every node that comes online pushes the Dead Zone boundary outward. Land that was monster-haunted wilderness becomes farmable, buildable, traversable. The 14 active nodes plus 104 plantings have reclaimed more safe territory than the Hearthcoast has seen since before the Sundering.

**Agricultural amplification.** The agricultural ward on Node 7 (Template #3) doubles crop yields in a 10-mile radius around Fenhollow. This isn't magic in the flashy sense — it's resonance-stabilized soil, optimized rainfall patterns, and pest suppression. The result is measurable: Fenhollow's grain surplus feeds Aelindra, supplements Stormcape's winter stores, and generates trade revenue.

**Monster suppression.** Active wards reduce creature condensation from boundary bleed. Fewer monsters means safer roads, safer farms, safer fishing. The Triangle Route's western leg was impossible before Node 5's reconstruction — the waters were too dangerous. Now it's a routine 2-day sail.

**Trade route safety.** Every trade route in the network passes through ward-protected territory. The Deep Road runs through underground ward coverage. The coastal routes benefit from the outer ring. Safe routes mean lower insurance costs (for those cities that have insurance guilds), faster transit, and more frequent runs.

### Priority Initiative — The Mirren River Barge & Ferry System

*Added by Zaryth Vel'quinar, 25th Frostmere, 1256 AS. "Greymoor has been bleeding for a decade. The Iron Road freezes shut three months a year and the miners pay the price. The Mirren River is right there — it's been the answer all along."*

**The Problem:** Greymoor (pop. ~7,000) is the most vulnerable major city on the Hearthcoast. Its sole trade link — the Iron Road to Harrowgate — becomes impassable from Greymist through Greenthaw (3–4 months). During winter, grain prices in Greymoor spike 40–60%, lamp oil doubles, and the miners' quarter runs on stored provisions and prayer. Raw ore leaves Greymoor at 3–5 gp/ton; finished tools sell in Harrowgate at 30–50 gp. The city captures almost none of that value.

**The Solution:** The Mirren River flows from the Greypeak foothills southeast through Harrowgate to the coast near Duskport. It is already the primary inland trade artery for the central Hearthcoast. But the upper Mirren — from Greymoor to Harrowgate — is currently unused for freight. The river narrows and shallows in the foothills, with seasonal rapids during snowmelt and low water in late summer.

**Proposed System — Three Stages:**

**Stage 1: Upper Mirren Flatboat Service (Greymoor ↔ Harrowgate) — ~800 gp**

| Component | Cost | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| 3 shallow-draft flatboats (12 ft, 1-ton capacity) | ~450 gp | Flat-bottomed, poled/rowed. Designed for shallow foothills water. Bram can oversee construction. |
| River survey (Upper Mirren, rapids mapping) | ~50 gp | Aldren Voss + Corwin. Map navigable stretches, portage points, seasonal depth changes. |
| 2 portage stations (rapids bypass) | ~150 gp | Simple docks + rollers at impassable stretches. Mule teams for short hauls around rapids. |
| Crew (6 rivermen, recruited from Harrowgate) | ~150 gp/year | 2 per boat. Local knowledge essential. Wages: ~2.5 gp/week each. |

**Capacity:** 3 boats × 1 ton × 2 runs/week = ~6 tons/week (good weather), ~2 tons/week (winter, reduced schedule).
**Transit time:** Greymoor → Harrowgate: ~1.5 days downstream (current-assisted). Harrowgate → Greymoor: ~2.5 days upstream (poled/rowed).
**Compared to Iron Road:** 5 days by charter carriage (when passable). The river cuts transit by 50–70% and operates year-round — the Mirren doesn't freeze until mid-Deepwinter, and even then the lower stretches stay open.

**What moves downstream (Greymoor → Harrowgate):**
- Iron ore, silver ore, refined metals
- Greypeak stone (building material)
- Mountain herbs (high-altitude medicinals)
- Eventually: finished tools and dark steel goods (once the finishing works are built — Tier 2, #11)

**What moves upstream (Harrowgate → Greymoor):**
- Grain, flour, preserved food (critical winter supply)
- Lamp oil, candles, heating fuel
- Textiles, leather goods, tools
- Medicine, alchemy supplies (from Dawnwatch line)
- Intercontinental goods (fire opals, sea-silk, singing granite — via Tidemeet → Harrowgate → upriver)

**Stage 2: Harrowgate River Dock & Warehouse — ~400 gp**

| Component | Cost | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| River dock (Mirren, Harrowgate north bank) | ~250 gp | Loading/unloading point. Connects flatboat service to existing Harrowgate markets and overland routes. |
| Small warehouse (staging) | ~150 gp | Goods staging for upriver/downriver transfer. Managed by existing Dawnwatch Trading Company contacts. |

This creates a **Tidemeet → (ship) → Duskport/coast → (river barge) → Harrowgate → (flatboat) → Greymoor** supply chain. Intercontinental goods, Kapuluan fire opals, Dawnwatch alchemy — all of it can reach Greymoor by water.

**Stage 3: Resonance-Assisted River Transport (Future — Lattice Engineering) — Cost TBD**

Once Phase 3 Lattice Engineering is complete, the flatboats can be fitted with resonance drives (like *Eilistraee's Grace*). This would:
- Eliminate the upstream speed penalty (2.5 days → 1.5 days)
- Enable winter operation even in partial ice conditions
- Reduce crew needs (resonance-assisted steering)
- Eventually: a dedicated Greymoor ↔ Tidemeet express service (river mouth to foothills, ~3 days)

Node 8 ("River Memory," Upper Mirren, 71%) sits directly on the route. A ward-enhanced river corridor would keep the water navigable longer into winter and reduce ice formation.
