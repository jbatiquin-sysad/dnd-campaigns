# Session 2 — The Civic Trust

**Date:** 2026-03-29
**DM:** Mara Solenne, The Storyteller
**Duration:** 10 turns

## Summary

Session 2 opened at dawn in the Temple of the Balanced Scale, the party exhausted but intact, the Partition Writ in Aldric's hands. A rooftop watcher with a signal mirror had already relayed their position to House Aldenmere — the window was shorter than expected.

Zaryth neutralized the watcher (Mira nat 20 stealth, non-lethal) and discovered a coded ledger with three signals already sent — including one that said only *"Edric."* House Aldenmere knew their second son was connected.

The party split: Group A (Aldric, Sylvara, Thessaly) to Magistrate Sera Voss with the Partition Writ. Group B (Zaryth, Mira, Orindel, Bram, Pip) to warn Lord Edric Aldenmere before his family could reach him.

Group B extracted Edric through the back door of his townhouse as his brother Calder arrived at the front — asking not for Edric, but for Harwick, the family solicitor. Harwick turned out to be a ten-year double agent working alongside Vael Orun. He had already burned the Vel Noctis contracts but kept a deed of civic trust — signed by the original Aldenmere patriarch 200 years ago, acknowledging the Partition Writ's authority. Proof the family always knew.

At the council chambers, the full party reunited. Harwick walked into the emergency session House Aldenmere's allies had called to preempt the Writ. Zaryth presented the evidence. Sylvara authenticated the Writ. Harwick testified from memory. Then Edric stood up — unprompted — and testified against his own family. Nat 20. The room broke.

Magistrate Voss suspended the session, convened an emergency civic inquiry, and froze House Aldenmere's harbor acquisitions. Outside, Thessaly held Calder's two men at the door alone when Mira and Bram both failed, until Voss's clerk emerged with city watch.

The city was saved. The harbor was protected. Voss's final words to the party: *"I'll need statements from all of you. But not today."*
