## 2. Place Names by Era

Pre-colonial place names often referenced physical features, spirits, or qualities and were largely in local languages. [web:31][web:33][web:29]

**Sample pre-colonial place names**  
- Barangay Malaya (Free Village)  
- Gubat-Diwata (Forest of Spirits)  
- Baybay-Amihan (Northeast Wind Shore)  
- Kabundukan ng Kidlat (Mountains of Lightning)  
- Ilog-Liwayway (River of Dawn)  
- Pulong Mutya (Isle of the Talisman)  
- Lawa ng Bituin (Lake of Stars)  
- Kuta-Bangkaw (Fort of Spears)  
- Balangay Lakas-Dagat (Sea-Strong Boat Village)  
- Punong Luwalhati (Tree of Glory)  
- Bukid-Sinag (Field of Rays)

These can be shortened or combined (e.g., “Malaya Gubat,” “Amihan Baybay”) to taste.

## 2.6 Regional Variations (Ethnolinguistic Groups)

Different regions in the Philippines have unique naming conventions and linguistic roots.

### Ilocano (Northern Luzon)
- **Names:** Agbayani, Agpaoa, Bumanglag, Duldulao, Agani, Ayo, Sinamar.
- **Places:** Vigan, Banayoyo, Kabugao, Laoag, Ilocos.

### Cordillera (Northern Highlands / Igorot)
- **Names:** Ipugo (People of the Earth), Bodong (Peace Pact), Kalinga (Warrior/Headhunter), Biyag (Life), Aguid (Way), Guman.
- **Places:** Sagada (Mist), Bontoc, Banaue (Rice Terraces), Kalinga, Ifugao (The Hill People).

### Visayan (Central Philippines & Mindanao)
- **Names:** Cabahug, Calinao, Pepito, Daigon, Bulan (Moon), Sugbo.
- **Places:** Sugbo (Cebu), Bacolod (Hill), Iloilo, Dumaguete, Siquijor.

### Bicolano (Southeastern Luzon)
- **Names:** Sawangan, Salvacion, Bicol, Vargas, Magayon (Beautiful).
- **Places:** Sawangan (Legazpi), Naga, Albay, Catanduanes, Donsol.

### Mindanao (Southern Philippines)
- **Names:** Mohammad, Abdul, Fatima, Sittie, Omar, Datu, Mutya.
- **Places:** Kuta Wato (Stone Fort/Cotabato), Zamboanga (City of Flowers), Davao, Butuan, Camiguin.

**Suffixes / types**
