# Group Portrait — The Dawnwatch Meets the Last Warden at Greyveil Pass
**Timeline:** 18th Highsun, 1247 AS — Day 108
**Scene:** Zaryth and Vaelith face each other at the entrance to Greyveil Pass. The 7 other party members stand behind Zaryth in the background, slightly out of focus.

---

```
SCENE: Two figures face each other in the foreground at the entrance to a
narrow mountain pass. Behind them, seven armed companions stand in a loose
group, slightly out of focus (depth of field effect). The pass is flanked
by grey stone walls. Carved into the eastern wall, twenty feet tall, is a
massive symbol: a closed eye — ancient, weathered, lichen-covered. The sky
is overcast, grey, heavy. Wind whips cloaks and hair. The ground is barren
highland scrub and ancient fitted stone paving. Beyond the pass, the air
shimmers faintly — an almost-invisible barrier, flickering like heat haze,
tinged with pale blue. Cold, austere, monumental atmosphere. Fantasy
medieval setting, no modern elements.

LIGHTING: Overcast grey daylight. No direct sun. Cold ambient light. The
only warm tones come from faint blue-white ward energy — the shimmer
beyond the pass and faint glowing symbols on the ancient figure's staff.
The carved closed eye on the wall catches what little light there is.

COMPOSITION: Two-shot foreground, group background. Camera angle slightly
low, looking up at the two foreground figures to emphasize the scale of
the pass walls and the carved eye above them. Depth of field — the seven
background figures are recognizable but soft.

FOREGROUND — LEFT: ZARYTH VEL'QUINAR
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

RACE & BUILD:
Dark elf. Male. 5'5". Lean, wiry, athletic build. Skin: deep
obsidian-black — NOT grey, NOT purple. Pointed elven ears, long. Angular
sharp features, high cheekbones. Pupils slightly larger than human —
adapted to darkness.

FACE & HAIR:
Hair: stark white, shoulder-length, wind-blown, slightly tangled from
days of mountain travel. Eyes: lavender, faintly luminous in the grey
light. Expression: solemn, respectful, resolute — a man meeting someone
he deeply honors. No smile. Jaw set. Eyes steady. Road-worn — slight
shadows under the eyes, windburn on the cheeks.

CLOTHING & ARMOR:
Full interlocking metal ring armor (chain mail) covering torso, arms,
and legs — worn, scratched, well-maintained. Over the armor: a
weatherproofed dark wool travel cloak, hooded (hood down), whipping in
the wind. A small embroidered emblem inside the collar — a rising sun
with 8 rays and a broken chain (Dawnwatch insignia). Under the cloak,
a round wooden shield with metal rim slung across his back by a leather
strap. Road dust on boots and greaves. Everything looks USED — weeks of
mountain travel.

WEAPONS & EQUIPMENT:
Longsword sheathed at his left hip — well-maintained, leather-wrapped
grip. In his LEFT hand, held forward at chest height: a small brass
instrument with a crystal needle (the Ward Compass) — the needle
trembles, pointing toward the pass. On his RIGHT hand: a silver signet
ring (Dawnwatch). No other visible weapons.

NARRATIVE DETAILS:
The Ward Compass is the focal object between the two figures — Zaryth
holds it forward as a credential, not a weapon. His posture is upright,
shoulders back — a paladin's bearing, even road-worn. His obsidian skin
contrasts sharply with the grey stone and white hair. The lavender eyes
catch the faint blue ward-shimmer from beyond the pass.

POSE & MOOD:
Standing facing the ancient figure (Vaelith), three feet apart. Left
hand extended with the compass. Right hand at his side, open — not on
his weapon. Body language: respectful, open, equal. Not supplicant, not
aggressive. One warden greeting another.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FOREGROUND — RIGHT: VAELITH, LAST WARDEN OF THE VIGIL
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

RACE & BUILD:
Ancient humanoid of indeterminate race — could be human, could be
something older. Extremely old. Tall — 5'10" but stooped slightly with
age. Thin, gaunt, but not frail — wiry strength remains. The body of
someone who has endured far beyond a normal lifespan through will or
magic.

FACE & HAIR:
Face like weathered granite — deep lines carved by centuries, not
decades. Skin the color of old parchment — yellowed, thin, stretched
over sharp bones. White hair, thin and wispy, whipping in the wind.
Eyes: burning, intense, sharp — the only part of them that doesn't look
ancient. The eyes are pale blue-white, almost luminous, as if lit from
within by ward energy. No beard. Gaunt cheeks. A face that has watched
civilizations rise and fall from a mountaintop.

CLOTHING & ARMOR:
Heavy robes — dark grey, almost black, layered, hooded (hood pushed
back). The robes are ancient but intact — patched, re-stitched, faded
from centuries of sun and wind. No armor. A thick leather belt with a
small pouch. Simple boots, cracked and re-soled many times. A small
iron medallion on a chain around the neck — stamped with a closed eye
symbol.

WEAPONS & EQUIPMENT:
A tall staff in the right hand, planted on the stone ground. Dark
ironwood, iron-shod at the base. The top of the staff is carved with
ward symbols — geometric patterns that glow faintly blue-white, pulsing
slowly like a heartbeat. The staff is the only source of warm light in
the scene. In the left hand, held forward toward Zaryth: a small
leather-bound book — old, worn, the closed eye embossed on the cover.
Offering it.

NARRATIVE DETAILS:
Vaelith is handing the book (the activation sequence — the missing
piece of the ward repair ritual) to Zaryth. This is the moment of
transfer — a thousand years of solitary duty being shared for the first
time. Their expression is complex: relief, exhaustion, and something
that might be hope, seen for the first time in a very long while.

POSE & MOOD:
Standing facing Zaryth, staff planted, left hand extending the book.
Slightly stooped but dignified. The posture of someone setting down an
immense weight. Eyes locked on Zaryth's — measuring, and finding what
they were looking for.
```
