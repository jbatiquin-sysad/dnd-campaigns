# Portrait Prompts — Seren Ashvale & Torven Ashwick

## Seren Ashvale — Monk (Way of the Open Hand)

```
CHARACTER: Seren Ashvale
TIMELINE: Day 156, 1248 AS — Dawnwatch Homestead, after the western arc
SETTING: Homestead porch at sunset, warm golden light, looking west toward the coast she left behind

RACE & BUILD:
Human woman, late 50s. 5'6", lean and wiry — not thin from deprivation but hard from decades of physical discipline. Every muscle defined from 31 years of climbing cliffs, swimming tidal pools, and surviving alone. Weathered brown skin, sun-darkened and salt-roughened. Moves like water — still when still, fast when fast.

FACE & HAIR:
Silver-streaked dark hair cut short and practical — she cuts it herself with a knife. Sharp dark eyes that read weather, tides, and people with equal precision. A thin scar runs from her left temple to her jaw, old and clean — white against dark skin. Crow's feet from decades of squinting into sea wind. Expression: quiet, steady, the faintest trace of peace — a woman who carried something heavy for a very long time and finally set it down.

CLOTHING & ARMOR:
No armor — she doesn't wear any (Unarmored Defense). Simple weathered travelling clothes: dark linen shirt, salt-stained trousers, bare feet (she prefers it — 31 years on coastal rock). A worn leather belt. No cloak — she runs warm from decades of exposure.

WEAPONS & EQUIPMENT:
An iron belt knife at her hip — old, Vigil-made, rust-proof despite decades of salt air. 10 darts in a leather roll on her belt. On her left hand: a dark polished steel Dawnwatch signet ring (faintly glowing ward-lines). On her right: an iron purpose ring. Around her neck: a quartz amulet (faintly luminous). Under her shirt, visible at the collar: a closed-eye medallion on a chain — Warden Drell's, carried always.

NARRATIVE DETAILS:
A faded closed-eye mark on the back of her left hand — the Vigil symbol, tattooed decades ago. In her pocket (not visible but implied by the weight of her expression): a small carved stone turtle and a bog oak figure of a woman on a cliff. She looks west — toward Stormcape, toward the nodes she tended, toward the coast that made her who she is.

POSE & MOOD:
Standing on the porch, one hand resting on the railing, looking west at the sunset. Weight on one hip. Relaxed but alert — she still checks the sky every few minutes. The mood is earned peace — not happiness exactly, but the absence of the weight she carried for 31 years.

STYLE NOTES:
Grounded fantasy realism, warm sunset lighting, oil painting style, detailed weathering on skin and clothing, emphasis on the contrast between her hard-worn appearance and the softness of the moment. She looks like someone the sea tried to break and couldn't.
```

## Torven Ashwick — Druid (Circle of the Land — Coast)

```
CHARACTER: Torven Ashwick
TIMELINE: Day 156, 1248 AS — Dawnwatch Homestead, after the western arc
SETTING: Homestead herb garden at morning, soft spring light, writing in his journal

RACE & BUILD:
Half-elf man, early 30s. 5'9", thin — still recovering from 4 months of near-starvation on the Stormcape coast. Narrow shoulders, long fingers (scholar's hands). Skin is light olive with a faint elven luminosity — the half-elf heritage shows in the cheekbones and the slight point of his ears (less pronounced than a full elf). He's gaunt but filling out — the homestead is feeding him properly for the first time in months.

FACE & HAIR:
Dark brown hair, slightly wavy, grown out past his ears (no barber on a deserted coast for 4 months). Hazel-green eyes — bright, alert, always observing. Slightly pointed ears (half-elf — less prominent than Orindel's wood elf ears, but visible). Young face aged by hardship — he looks 35 despite being early 30s. A few days of stubble. Expression: earnest concentration — he's writing, and when he writes, the world disappears.

CLOTHING & ARMOR:
Behir scale-leather armor — blue-grey juvenile behir scales riveted over dark leather at the chest and shoulders, clearly handmade (Mira's work). A wooden shield leaned against the garden bench beside him (not worn — he's off duty). Archive scholar's travelling clothes underneath — linen shirt, practical trousers, resoled boots. A wooden staff carved with ward symbols leaned against his shoulder — his druidic focus.

WEAPONS & EQUIPMENT:
The carved wooden staff (druidic focus / quarterstaff) beside him. A dagger at his belt. On his left hand: a dark polished steel Dawnwatch signet ring (ward-lines faintly visible). On his right: an iron purpose ring. Around his neck: a quartz amulet. In his lap: an open journal, ink pot balanced on the bench arm, a calligrapher's pen in his right hand. He writes with precise, Archive-trained handwriting.

NARRATIVE DETAILS:
His journal is his third since joining the party — he documents everything. The behir scale armor is slightly too large — Mira fitted it for him but he's still underweight. A small sprig of coastal herb (storm kelp) is pressed between the journal pages as a bookmark. His eyes have the same shape as Orindel's — the Ashwick family resemblance, visible if you know to look.

POSE & MOOD:
Seated on the garden bench, journal open, writing. One leg tucked under him. The staff leaned against his shoulder. Morning light on his face. He's not looking at the viewer — he's looking at the page, absorbed. A small smile — he's writing something that matters to him. The mood is quiet purpose — a young man who survived something terrible and is making sense of it through words.

STYLE NOTES:
Grounded fantasy realism, soft morning light, oil painting style, detailed textures on the behir scale armor and journal. Emphasis on the contrast between his scholar's delicacy and the rough-hewn armor. He looks like an academic who walked through fire and came out the other side still holding his pen.
```
