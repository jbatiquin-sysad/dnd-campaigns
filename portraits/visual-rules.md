# D&D 5e Visual Rules — Race & Class Appearance Reference

Reference file for generating accurate character portrait prompts. All traits are per D&D 5e Basic Rules 2018.

---

## Racial Appearance

### Lightfoot Halfling
- **Height:** ~3 feet tall (90–100 cm)
- **Build:** Slight, lean, nimble
- **Feet:** BAREFOOT — large, tough leathery soles, thick curly hair on top of feet. Halflings do NOT wear boots or shoes.
- **Face:** Round, warm, bright eyes, button nose, expressive
- **Ears:** Slightly pointed (less than elves)
- **Hair:** Typically curly or wavy, brown/auburn/sandy tones common
- **Skin:** Ranges from fair to tan, ruddy cheeks common
- **Proportions:** Not just "short humans" — proportionally smaller with larger feet relative to body, slightly larger heads relative to torso
- **Movement:** Light on their feet, quiet, naturally stealthy

### Stout Halfling
- Same as Lightfoot except:
- **Build:** Stockier, broader shoulders, more muscular for their size
- **Face:** Slightly broader, more determined set to the jaw
- **Constitution:** Visibly hardier — thicker limbs, more compact frame
- **Feet:** Same as Lightfoot — BAREFOOT, tough soles, curly foot-hair

### Drow (Dark Elf)
- **Height:** 5'4"–5'7" (shorter than other elves)
- **Build:** Lean, wiry, athletic
- **Skin:** Obsidian black / deep charcoal / dark purple-black (NOT grey, NOT light purple)
- **Hair:** Stark white, silver-white, or pale yellow. Often worn long or in practical military styles
- **Eyes:** Lavender, pale lilac, silver, or red. Faintly luminous in low light
- **Ears:** Long, pointed (elven)
- **Face:** Angular, sharp features, high cheekbones
- **Expression:** Often guarded, intense — surface Drow carry visible wariness from a lifetime of distrust
- **Light sensitivity:** May squint or shade eyes in bright daylight scenes
- **Darkvision:** 120 ft — eyes adapted to darkness, pupils may appear larger

### High Elf
- **Height:** 5'5"–6'1"
- **Build:** Slender, graceful, almost ethereal
- **Skin:** Fair to bronze, sometimes with a faint golden or silver sheen
- **Hair:** Copper, silver, gold, black, or white — often long, well-maintained
- **Eyes:** Green, gold, silver, or blue — bright, alert, slightly larger than human
- **Ears:** Long, pointed, prominent
- **Face:** Angular, refined, symmetrical — an ageless quality
- **Age note:** Elves over 100 may show age only in their eyes, not their skin. A 130-year-old elf looks 30 but carries centuries in their gaze.

### Wood Elf
- **Height:** 5'4"–5'10"
- **Build:** Lean, athletic, built for movement through terrain
- **Skin:** Copper, olive, or light brown — often weathered/sun-touched
- **Hair:** Brown, black, or auburn — practical styles, often braided or tied back
- **Eyes:** Green, brown, or hazel — watchful, alert
- **Ears:** Long, pointed (elven)
- **Face:** Less angular than High Elf — warmer, more grounded features
- **Movement:** Fleet-footed (35 ft speed) — should look like a runner/tracker

### Hill Dwarf
- **Height:** 4'0"–4'6"
- **Build:** Broad, stocky, barrel-chested, dense muscle
- **Skin:** Fair to deep tan, weathered
- **Hair:** Thick, often braided. Beards are culturally significant — long, well-maintained, sometimes decorated
- **Eyes:** Dark, deep-set, steady
- **Face:** Broad, strong jaw, prominent brow ridge
- **Hands:** Thick, calloused, built for work
- **Proportions:** Wide shoulders, short legs, low center of gravity — built like a boulder

### Mountain Dwarf
- Same as Hill Dwarf except:
- **Build:** Even more muscular — trained for combat, not just labor
- **Armor comfort:** Mountain Dwarves are proficient in light and medium armor by race — they wear armor like a second skin
- **Bearing:** More martial posture, carries themselves like a soldier or noble

## Class Visual Cues

### Paladin
- Armor is central to identity — always armored, even at rest
- Holy symbol visible (worn, carried, or integrated into armor/shield)
- Weapon well-maintained — a paladin's blade reflects their discipline
- Bearing: upright, commanding, shoulders back — even a fallen paladin carries themselves like an officer
- Lay on Hands: hands may show faint warmth/glow in healing scenes

### Rogue
- Practical clothing — dark colors, fitted, nothing that catches or jingles
- Weapons concealed or close to body — daggers, short blades, hand crossbows
- Tools of the trade: thieves' tools, lockpicks, pouches
- Eyes always scanning — alert, calculating expression
- Movement: low center of gravity, ready to move

### Cleric (Life Domain)
- Holy symbol prominent — worn on chest, shield, or held
- Armor: Life Domain clerics wear heavy armor — chain mail, shields
- Healing hands: may show warmth or gentle light in healing scenes
- Expression: compassionate but steady — someone who has seen suffering and chosen to face it
- Prayer book or holy text often carried

### Wizard (Evocation)
- Spellbook is the most important possession — always nearby
- Arcane focus or component pouch visible
- Robes or practical scholar's clothing (not always robes — adventuring wizards dress for travel)
- Hands: may show faint arcane residue, ink stains (from spellbook work)
- Eyes: bright, curious, slightly intense — the look of someone who sees patterns others miss
- Evocation specialists: may have faint scorch marks, singed edges, or heat shimmer near hands

## Armor Visual Reference

| Armor | Visual Description |
|-------|-------------------|
| Padded | Quilted cloth layers, soft, bulky |
| Leather | Fitted leather chest piece + bracers, flexible |
| Studded Leather | Leather with metal rivets/studs across chest and shoulders |
| Chain Shirt | Metal rings woven into a shirt, worn under clothing or over tunic |
| Chain Mail | Full metal ring coverage — torso, arms, legs. Heavy, visible, loud |
| Scale Mail | Overlapping metal scales on leather backing — fish-scale pattern |
| Shield (wooden) | Round or kite-shaped, wood with metal rim, leather grip |
| Shield (steel) | Metal face, heavier, more reflective |

## General Notes for Image Generation

- **Scale matters:** When multiple characters appear together, height differences must be visible. A halfling's head reaches a dwarf's chest. A dwarf's head reaches an elf's shoulder.
- **Wear and tear:** Adventuring gear should look USED — oiled leather, polished-but-scratched metal, repaired straps, road dust. Not pristine.
- **Light sources:** Underground/night scenes should use warm lantern/torch light. Drow and elves see in darkness — their eyes may catch light differently.
- **Fantasy realism:** Aim for grounded fantasy — practical gear, real weight to weapons, believable postures. Not high-fantasy glamour.
- **Emotional state:** Characters should reflect their narrative moment — exhaustion after a long operation, quiet pride after a victory, tension before a breach.
