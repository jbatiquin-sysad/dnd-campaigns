## Personality
He will absolutely pick your pocket and donate the proceeds to an orphanage, and he sees no contradiction in this whatsoever. He quotes scripture with genuine reverence and then immediately uses it to justify something his goddess would probably frown at.

## Flaw
He rationalizes everything — no matter how questionable the action, he can construct a theological argument for why it was righteous.

## Bond
A network of street children he funds through his "redistributive ministry" — they're his eyes, ears, and the reason he keeps doing this.

## Ideal
Good ends justify creative means. The gods care about outcomes, not methods.

## Backstory
Orindel received a genuine divine calling at age sixty — a vision of his goddess asking him to care for the forgotten — but the temple turned him away for his criminal record. He serves his goddess independently, funding charitable works through burglary, smuggling, and the occasional confidence scheme.

The party's solo rescue specialist and healer. Rescued Vael Orun from behind the altar at the Temple of the Balanced Scale. Executed the Maren Holt rescue alone — broke the padlock, neutralized the guard, found her alive after 3 months captive, healed her, extracted her to safety. Delivered Maret Soll's sealed letter to Veyra Solund — watched her grief turn into resolve. Ran street clinics in every city, recruiting key allies through genuine care. Coordinated the pigeon network. Named the pigeon Tomas — after a boy he lost.

Served as chamber healer during the Valis Procedure. Used both spell slots — Healing Word on Sylvara at minute 4, Healing Word on Aldric at minute 8 — then stood for six more minutes with no magic left. When Pip was breaking at minute 12, Orindel put his hand on his shoulder. No spell. Just presence. That was enough.

His darkest hour: a boy named Tomas, age nine, third name on his list. Found him too late. Fever. The clinic was closed — holy day. One spell slot wasn't enough. "The light isn't a place you return to. It's a direction you choose. Every morning."

At the Dawnwatch Homestead, Orindel established 3 beehives, planted the garden with Mira, and placed the carved Marsh Light icon from Sister Venn on the mantelpiece above Aldric's hearth. "For the house." Nobody objected. It looked right.

Composed the Ward-Light Hymn at Greyveil Pass — three verses and a chorus, inspired by the Node 12 repair and Vaelith's 114-year vigil. The hymn became integral to every subsequent repair, providing sympathetic resonance that stabilized the anchors and reduced feedback. Taught Pip to play the flute — "You don't force the sound out. You offer it." At Sethrak's Threshold, when his spell slots were spent and his anchor was failing, he sang the hymn to hold the channel — channeling the ward's frequency through music alone. It worked. It wasn't in the manual. Coached Pip through five-note scales, connected phrases, and the full melody. By Node 10, Pip played every note perfect. Orindel heard the ward singing through the stone road, harmonized with it by campfire, and tuned a resonance crystal by ear at the Warden's Forge. His voice isn't trained. It doesn't matter.

## Defining Moment
Sang the Ward-Light Hymn through the Anchor Point aperture and the Vel'thara — an entire civilization of beings made of crystallized sound — harmonized with him in real time. Then composed The Blacktide Ode on the deck of a ship, four voices, no instruments, about the battle and the choice to show mercy. His hymn is now being sung across twelve civilizations on the other side of the boundary.

## Downtime
Composes. A new piece, untitled, built with Pip — about a door, a flower, and three thousand years of silence. Tends Wren Hale's memorial stone in the garden every morning. Teaches at the school. His palm still has a faint burn scar from ring-humming underwater at Node 13.
