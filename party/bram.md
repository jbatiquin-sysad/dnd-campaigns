## Key Features
- Spellcasting (INT-based): Spellbook, prepare INT mod + level spells/day. 4x 1st-level, 2x 2nd-level slots.
- 2nd-level spells: Scorching Ray, Web, Misty Step
- Arcane Recovery: Recover spell slots 1/day after short rest
- Evocation Savant: Halve gold and time to copy evocation spells
- Sculpt Spells: When casting evocation, choose up to 1+spell level creatures to automatically save and take no damage
- Lucky: Reroll 1s on d20
- Brave: Advantage vs. frightened
- Stout Resilience: Advantage vs. poison, resistance to poison damage
- Military Rank: Soldiers of former unit recognize and defer to his rank
- Athletics + Intimidation proficiencies

## Personality
He barks orders at a volume completely disproportionate to his 3-foot frame and gets genuinely offended when people don't snap to attention. He found a dead wizard's spellbook on a battlefield and taught himself magic through sheer stubborn repetition — he treats spells like military drills.

## Flaw
He refuses to retreat or admit defeat, even when tactically it's the only sensible option.

## Bond
His old unit was wiped out in an ambush he survived only because he was separated from them — he carries their insignia and won't rest until he knows who ordered it. The regiment was formally dissolved afterward, and Bram was discharged. Not dishonor. Just a unit that no longer existed and an army that had no place for a sergeant without soldiers.

## Ideal
Discipline wins wars. Chaos gets people killed. Everything has a proper order and a proper time.

## Backstory
Bram served fifteen years as a halfling infantry sergeant, famous for being the loudest soldier in any regiment and the last one standing in any brawl. His unit was wiped out in an ambush he survived only because he'd been separated from them on a scouting detail. The regiment was dissolved afterward — no unit, no soldiers, no purpose. He was formally discharged. Not dishonor. Just irrelevance. He found a scorched spellbook in the ruins of that same battle and spent two years deciphering it by candlelight, driven by the conviction that magic was just another weapon that needed proper training. He sat in a Duskport tavern for three days after discharge until Thessaly found him and said "You look like someone who's good at things and has nothing to be good at." That was the door back.

In Session 2, Bram stayed in Duskport with Thessaly to guard three captives in Magistrate Voss's custody and support the inquiry into House Aldenmere. In Session 3, he received a pigeon message from Zaryth — "Come to Aelindra immediately. You are needed for a four-point evocation procedure. Non-negotiable." He boarded the fastest ship he could find and sailed three days on a vessel that smelled like fish oil. He arrived on Day 6 — hours before the operation — received a full primer on the mission, and said "I'm ready."

On the night of the operation, Bram held the east arc of the Valis Procedure grounding circle for the full 14 minutes. He never flickered once. Not once. When Pip's arc collapsed at minute 12, Bram shifted east and absorbed overflow without being asked. When Sylvara faltered at minute 4, he compensated. His hands trembled for an hour afterward, but his quadrant never dimmed. He also corrected one of Sylvara's calibration glyphs during circle setup — she accepted it without hesitation.

On the voyage home, he shared his dream: building a school for talented kids with no money. His aspiration: to be a leader, not just a commander — someone people follow because they trust him. His darkest hour: military discharge — not dishonor, just irrelevance. He sat in a Duskport tavern for three days until Thessaly found him and said "You look like someone who's good at things and has nothing to be good at." That was the door back.

In Session 4, Bram coordinated the harbor interdiction in Duskport — working with Sergeant Harren to establish a watch cordon, raising the harbor chain, and securing the ship carrying the three escaped captives. All three recaptured, ship seized, escape route closed. He also used his quarterstaff to non-lethally stop the Rooftop Watcher from running. In Harrowgate, he worked dock labor for 2 days gathering intel on Warehouse 6 and the night crew, identified Harsk (ex-military night crew boss), and stood alongside Zaryth and Aldric during the warehouse confrontation where 11 were detained and a full weapons shipment seized. He arrived in Aelindra on Day 6 after a 3-day solo voyage on a ship that smelled like fish oil, received a full primer, and said "I'm ready." He held the east arc of the Valis Procedure without flickering once.

In Greymoor, Bram secured Shaft 9's main gate alongside Thessaly and six volunteer miners (Intimidation 19). He also recruited Torben Vask — the former Shaft 9 crew chief — through dock labor cover, earning the man's trust over arm-wrestling matches he kept losing. Bram coordinated the gate team's timing with the underground strike, ensuring no reinforcements entered and no one escaped.

In Salthollow, Bram confirmed wagon tracks on the east road — a two-stage relay at a half-mile turnout (Perception 16). During Operation Athenaeum, he led the wagon interception at the staging turnout alongside Thessaly and Pip (Intimidation 20). Seven crates seized, two drivers secured, zero combat.

In Fenhollow, Bram mapped the militia stockade's patrol patterns and identified the 12 imported soldiers drilling Thornwatch formations (Perception 20). He confirmed Ashdale was frustrated — the wardhouse door wouldn't open without the fragment. At dawn, Bram stood at the stockade gate with Thessaly and Captain Brenn. When Brenn walked through in his old tabard, every local militiaman stood down. The 12 imports surrendered when they saw the full party converging from three directions.

In Veldmere, Bram worked the south wharves gathering fleet intelligence — confirming 4 ships provisioning for departure, Warehouse 3 sealed with armed guards, and night crew activity. During Operation Veldmere, he entered Warehouse 3 with Sylvara during the guard changeover (3-minute window) and carried all 3 remaining amplifier cylinders (120 lbs) out through back alleys. Zero detection.

Bram smelted seized enemy weapons into 6 steel ingots at Old Maren's forge in Veldmere. He later commissioned the 8 Dawnwatch signet rings from Master Orin Ashvale in Aelindra — using 2 of those ingots. "Their steel becomes our rings. What was used to threaten becomes what holds us together." Thessaly remembered that line. She told everyone she wouldn't tell.

At the Dawnwatch Homestead, Bram took charge of construction — stripping the rotted roof, shaping replacement beams, building the table for ten. He cleared the north meadow for the camping ground and is planning Phase 2: the school. "Flat land. Room for a building. Kids from the marsh villages could walk to a school." Thessaly will handle the budget and the curriculum. They share a room now. They don't discuss it. It just happened.
