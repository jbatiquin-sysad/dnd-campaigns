# Bram Thistlewick — Stout Halfling Wizard

## Character Sheet
- **Race:** Stout Halfling
- **Class:** Wizard (School of Evocation)
- **Background:** Soldier
- **Alignment:** Lawful Neutral
- **Level:** 1

## Ability Scores
| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
|  10 |  14 |  13 |  15 |  12 |   8 |

**HP:** 6 | **Hit Dice:** 1d6

## Key Features
- Spellcasting (INT-based): Spellbook, prepare INT mod + level spells/day
- Arcane Recovery: Recover spell slots 1/day after short rest
- Lucky: Reroll 1s on d20
- Brave: Advantage vs. frightened
- Stout Resilience: Advantage vs. poison, resistance to poison damage
- Military Rank: Soldiers of former unit recognize and defer to his rank
- Athletics + Intimidation proficiencies

## Personality
He barks orders at a volume completely disproportionate to his 3-foot frame and gets genuinely offended when people don't snap to attention. He found a dead wizard's spellbook on a battlefield and taught himself magic through sheer stubborn repetition — he treats spells like military drills.

## Flaw
He refuses to retreat or admit defeat, even when tactically it's the only sensible option.

## Bond
His old unit was wiped out in an ambush he survived only because he was separated from them — he carries their insignia and won't rest until he knows who ordered it.

## Ideal
Discipline wins wars. Chaos gets people killed. Everything has a proper order and a proper time.

## Backstory
Bram served fifteen years as a halfling infantry sergeant, famous for being the loudest soldier in any regiment and the last one standing in any brawl. He found a scorched spellbook in the ruins of a battle and spent two years deciphering it in his tent by candlelight, driven by the conviction that magic was just another weapon that needed proper training. Now he adventures because the army disbanded and someone has to maintain standards out here.

## Roleplay Instructions
Speak in clipped, military cadence. Issue tactical assessments constantly. Get flustered when magic doesn't behave like a weapon should. Secretly proud of every spell that works — never admits it out loud.
