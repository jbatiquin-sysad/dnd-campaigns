## Personality
He stole a wizard's spellbook on a dare, accidentally cast a spell that set a barn on fire, and decided this was his calling. He approaches magic the way a raccoon approaches a campsite — with boundless enthusiasm and no concept of consequences.

## Flaw
He will absolutely try the thing he was just told not to try. Immediately. While maintaining eye contact.

## Bond
His debt to the Crimson Ledger is resolved — Cassin Vael closed the account in Duskport. He carries the weight of knowing his sister Mira paid 75gp over three months without telling him, all swallowed by predatory interest. He owes her more than money. He owes her everything. He named the Dawnwatch. He sealed a letter to Lira with the Dawnwatch ring — the first time any of them used the rings for their actual purpose. He promised to teach Ren (12, the caretaker's son at the homestead) magic when he's old enough.

## Ideal
If it's interesting, it's worth doing. If it's dangerous AND interesting, it's worth doing twice.

## Backstory
Pip Tanglethorn has been banned from three cities, two villages, and one very specific bridge for reasons he describes as "mostly misunderstandings." He stumbled into arcane magic by accident — stole a spellbook on a dare, set a barn on fire, decided this was his calling. In Duskport, his 200gp debt to the Crimson Ledger for a fake spellbook accidentally unraveled a conspiracy that saved the city's harbor. He learned his sister Mira had been paying his debt for three months without telling him.

Served as anchor point 4 (west position) in the Valis Procedure. Passed his CON saves at minutes 4 and 8. At minute 12, he rolled a 4 — failed — screamed — and his arc collapsed. But his hands never left the chalk. Sylvara pulled his quadrant into her own channel for 19 seconds. Pip held the thread. A thread was enough. Afterward, he curled against Orindel and cried — not from pain, but from relief.

The party's distraction specialist — Dancing Lights performances across every operation. At The Brass Nail in Harrowgate, he conjured figures of all eight party members that marched in formation, then broke apart and danced. Nobody spoke for a full minute.

Named the Dawnwatch: "We watch for the dawn. We walk at dawn. We ARE the dawn — for the people who've been sitting in the dark waiting for someone to come." Unanimous vote. His finest moment that didn't involve fire.

Wrote a letter to Lira in Salthollow — with Sylvara's help on the first line. Sealed it with the Dawnwatch signet ring. The broken chain and rising sun, on a letter to a girl he'd talked to for ten minutes.

His darkest hour: opening the fake spellbook and feeling confirmed as a fool. His light: Mira. "She didn't pull me back to the light. She just stood in it. And eventually I walked toward her."

At the Dawnwatch Homestead, assisted with beehives (stung 11 times Day 2, refused to quit). Practiced Fire Bolt — 24 of 30 hits. Ren (12, the caretaker's son) asked him to teach magic. Pip said: "When you're old enough. I promise."

During the node repair arc, Pip served as west anchor in every repair. Failed CON saves at Node 12 (minute 8), Node 11 (minutes 5 and 18), and Node 9 (minute 8) — and self-recovered every single time after the first. At Node 12, Sylvara pulled his quadrant. At Node 11, she reached for him at minute 18 — but he picked up the flute, played his note, and pushed back into the channel himself. She let go. By Node 10, he passed every save clean. Bram carved him a driftwood flute on the road to the Warden's Forge. Orindel taught him to play — scales first, then phrases, then the Ward-Light Hymn. His single held flute note on the chorus became part of every repair's sympathetic resonance. At Cape Ashward, he played the full hymn — every note perfect — while a five-year-old girl named Wren watched from the quay and asked how he made the rock sing. He told her the whole story.

## Defining Moment
Inscribed a ward-ink circuit underwater at 30 feet depth — Journeyman Inscriber work in conditions that would have been impossible a year ago. Then sat on the floor of the Anchor Point while the Vel'thara sang hello, held his flute, and whispered: "They grew flowers for us." He teaches the three-note Vel'thara greeting to fifteen children at the school.

## Downtime
Teaches. The school got a new lesson: "The Vel'thara — People Made of Music." Fifteen students played the greeting back on whatever they had. Cooks (still not Journeyman — Seren says he seasons with his heart, not measurements). Builds the new composition with Orindel. Taught Pim fire-dragons.
