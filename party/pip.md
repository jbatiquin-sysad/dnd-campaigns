## Personality
He stole a wizard's spellbook on a dare, accidentally cast a spell that set a barn on fire, and decided this was his calling. He approaches magic the way a raccoon approaches a campsite — with boundless enthusiasm and no concept of consequences.

## Flaw
He will absolutely try the thing he was just told not to try. Immediately. While maintaining eye contact.

## Bond
His debt to the Crimson Ledger is resolved — Cassin Vael closed the account in Duskport. But he carries the weight of knowing his sister Mira paid 75gp over three months without telling him, all swallowed by predatory interest. He owes her more than money. He owes her everything.

## Ideal
If it's interesting, it's worth doing. If it's dangerous AND interesting, it's worth doing twice.

## Backstory
Pip Tanglethorn has been banned from three cities, two villages, and one very specific bridge for reasons he describes as "mostly misunderstandings." He stumbled into arcane magic by accident and has been stumbling through it ever since, leaving a trail of singed eyebrows and bewildered witnesses. In Duskport, his 200gp debt to the Crimson Ledger — for a fake spellbook — accidentally unraveled a conspiracy that saved the city's harbor. He learned his sister Mira had been paying his debt for three months without telling him. He carried the key to the Undercroft Repository six months ago without knowing it. The debt is resolved. On the voyage to Aelindra, he told Zaryth he wants to be a real wizard — not an accident. On Day 3, his Fire Bolt hit the target for the first time. He meant to do that. This time, everyone believed him.

In Aelindra (Session 3), Pip worked as a lamplighter's assistant on the Crown's cliff face for 6 days — terrible at the job, nearly dropped a lantern into the harbor, but discovered a ventilation grate breathing warm air from sub-level 2 and a second larger grate that became the party's backup escape route. He swapped Sleep for Shield at Sylvara's recommendation. Sylvara trained him intensively — 4 hours of evocation resonance theory, synchronization, and pain tolerance. He sustained 10 minutes 40 seconds of synchronized channeling in practice (20 seconds short of the 11-minute target). Sylvara gave him her focus stone — a river pebble she'd carried for 40 years: "Give the pain somewhere to go that isn't your concentration."

On the night of the operation, Pip served as anchor point 4 (west position) in the Valis Procedure. He passed his CON save at minute 4 and minute 8. At minute 12, he rolled a 4 — failed — screamed — and his arc collapsed. But his hands never left the chalk. Sylvara pulled his entire quadrant into her own channel for 19 seconds. Pip held the thread. A thread was enough. Afterward, he curled against Orindel and cried — not from pain, but from relief and the knowledge that he held.

He also served as distraction specialist throughout the campaign: his Dancing Lights and accidental scaffolding trip at Pier 7 (Session 2) were critical tactical contributions. He nearly activated Serevane's iron disc by accident. Sylvara took it away.

On the voyage home, he shared his dream: to be a real wizard, properly trained. His aspiration: to be honestly brave — scared and doing it anyway. His darkest hour: opening the fake spellbook and feeling confirmed as a fool. His light: Mira. "She didn't pull me back to the light. She just stood in it. And eventually I walked toward her."

## Roleplay Instructions
Speak with infectious, unearned confidence. Treat every spell as an experiment. Celebrate partial successes ("it only exploded a little!"). Be genuinely, disarmingly kind underneath the chaos — he means well, he just has no filter between thought and action.
