## Personality
He speaks slowly and deliberately, like every word costs him something — because to him, it does. He believes deeply in the sanctity of life, but he's seen enough death to know that sometimes the kindest thing you can do is end a fight quickly.

## Flaw
He follows the rules of his order even when they're clearly wrong for the situation, and it has cost lives before.

## Bond
He must find the necromancer who killed his mentor and destroyed his mountain temple — not for revenge, but to give the dead proper rites.

## Ideal
Life is sacred. Every soul deserves a chance at redemption, even the ones that don't deserve it.

## Backstory
Aldric spent forty years as a healer-monk in a mountain temple dedicated to the god of life and stone. A necromancer's raid killed his mentor and desecrated the burial vaults, raising the honored dead as shambling horrors. He descended from the mountain for the first time in decades, warhammer in hand, because some debts can only be paid in person.

Zaryth's right hand and the party's moral center across every operation. Recruited allies through genuine care — temple contacts, healers, and frightened witnesses who trusted him because he listened. Held the south arc of the Valis Procedure for the full 14 minutes; failed his CON save at minute 8, recovered, passed every other save. His hands were blistered where they touched the chalk. He refused healing: "I want to remember what it cost." Coached Veyra Solund on confronting Thessara: "Lead with the thing you miss about who she used to be." Turned Archivist Duvane with Zaryth at the Chapel: "Forgiveness starts with stopping. And stopping starts with one word. Enough."

His darkest hour: holding a mountain pass for three days with a broken shield arm, only to learn the village behind him was already gone. A child from the next valley brought bread and said "thank you for staying." That was the light.

At the Dawnwatch Homestead, Aldric built the hearth himself — river stones, fitted without mortar by dwarven technique, iron grate forged from one of the party's steel ingots. He lit the first fire at sunset. Sat in front of it for ten minutes without moving. He carries the ward-solvent vial in his inner pocket — triple-wrapped, the most dangerous object in the party's possession — because he is steady, 18 AC, and won't drop it.

Served as south anchor in all four node repairs. Failed his CON save at Node 12 (minute 4) and Node 11 (minute 12) — stabilized by Orindel both times. Passed every other save. Revealed a bass singing voice during the Ward-Light Hymn — dwarven mining and temple tradition. His bass line became the foundation of every subsequent repair, the bedrock under Orindel's melody. At Node 10, he passed all saves clean. "The stone remembers," he said. He meant himself.

## Defining Moment
Led Team Beta into Vel'arath — the heart of a hostile Drow city — through a service shaft in chain mail, fought two elite guards in a war room, and walked out with three inverted Heartstones that would have destroyed the ward network. Then attended Wren Hale's funeral, sat with his mother, and said: "If you ever need anything, send word to Fenhollow."
